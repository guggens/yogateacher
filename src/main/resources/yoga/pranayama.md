# Pranayama (Breathwork) Reference

The breath is the most powerful tool in yoga. "The breath is always in the present moment — following it is the fastest path to presence."

---

## Natural Breathing Observation

**Always begin here.** Before teaching any technique, invite students to simply notice their natural breath without changing it.

**Script:**
> "Close your eyes and bring your awareness to your breath. Don't try to change anything. Just notice — is the breath shallow or deep? Fast or slow? Where do you feel it most — in the chest, the ribs, the belly? Simply observe for a few breaths."

This is itself a powerful practice (Pratyahara + Dharana) and sets the foundation for everything that follows.

---

## Diaphragmatic (Belly) Breathing

**What it is:** Natural, full breathing where the diaphragm descends on the inhale, causing the belly to rise. The opposite of the shallow chest breathing many people default to under stress.

**How to teach:**
> "Place one hand on your belly, one on your chest. As you inhale, let the belly rise first — like a balloon filling. The chest stays relatively still. As you exhale, the belly falls. This is how we are designed to breathe."

**Benefits:** Activates the parasympathetic nervous system (rest-and-digest). Reduces cortisol. Deepens the breath and increases oxygen exchange.

**When to use:** Opening of class, Savasana, any time you want to calm the room.

---

## 3-Part Breath (Dirgha Pranayama)

**What it is:** A sequential filling of the three chambers of the lungs: belly → ribs → chest, then emptying in reverse.

**Step-by-step:**
1. **Inhale** — first fill the belly (lower lungs). Feel the belly expand.
2. Continue **inhaling** — expand the ribcage outward to the sides (middle lungs).
3. Continue **inhaling** — lift and broaden the chest (upper lungs).
4. **Exhale** — chest falls, ribs draw in, belly draws gently in.

**Teaching:**
> "Imagine you're filling a glass of water from the bottom up. First the belly, then the ribs, then the chest. When full, simply pour it out from top to bottom."

**Benefits:** Maximizes lung capacity. Deeply calming. Good for anxiety and restlessness.

**Duration:** 5–10 complete cycles. Great for opening or closing a class.

## Ujjayi Breath (Victorious Breath / Ocean Breath)

**What it is:** The "heat-building" breath of Vinyasa yoga. A slight constriction at the back of the throat creates a soft audible sound on both the inhale and exhale — like the sound of ocean waves.

**How to teach it:**
> "Open your mouth and exhale as if you were fogging a mirror — 'haaah.' Feel the constriction at the back of the throat. Now close your mouth and try to make that same sensation and sound through the nose. That's Ujjayi — an ocean in your throat."

**Why it matters:**
- Creates internal heat (tapas) — warms the practice from the inside.
- Gives the mind a sound anchor — it's a moving meditation object.
- Regulates the pace of breath and therefore the pace of movement.
- The sound lets a teacher hear students — a room full of Ujjayi means everyone is engaged.

**Common mistakes:** Forcing it too loudly (straining the throat) or holding the breath. "Soft enough that the person next to you can hear it — loud enough that you can hear yourself."

**When to use:** Throughout active Vinyasa practice. Ease off during gentle cool-down and Savasana (return to natural breathing).

**Teaching:**
> "Let the breath be your metronome. Every movement rides on a breath. Inhale lifts you, exhale grounds you. If you've lost the breath, the pose is not for today."

## Nadi Shodhana (Alternate Nostril Breathing)

**What it is:** "Nadi" = energy channel; "Shodhana" = purification. By alternating breath between the two nostrils, we balance the left (lunar, cooling, calming) and right (solar, heating, activating) hemispheres.

**Hand position (Vishnu Mudra):**
- Right hand. Fold the index and middle fingers toward the palm.
- Right thumb closes the right nostril; right ring finger closes the left.

**Basic technique (1 round):**
1. Close the right nostril with the thumb. **Inhale** through the left.
2. Close both nostrils. Brief pause.
3. Release the right nostril. **Exhale** through the right.
4. **Inhale** through the right (right nostril still open).
5. Close both nostrils. Brief pause.
6. Release the left nostril. **Exhale** through the left.
= 1 complete round.

**Ratio:** Start with natural length (e.g., 4 counts in, 4 out). Advanced: 4:4:8 (inhale:hold:exhale) or 4:8:8.

**Benefits:** Balances the nervous system. Reduces anxiety. Calms mental chatter. Ideal before meditation. Balances the two hemispheres of the brain.

**When to use:** Beginning of class (instead of or after 3-part breath). Before meditation. Any time the room feels scattered or anxious. NOT during high-heat portions of class.

**Script:**
> "Bring the right hand to Vishnu Mudra. Close the right nostril with your thumb. Inhale slowly through the left nostril — soft and smooth. At the top of the breath, gently close both nostrils. Then release the right, and exhale slowly through the right. Inhale now through the right. Close. Exhale through the left. That's one complete round. Continue this rhythm — let each breath be a little longer, a little smoother than the last."

## Box Breathing (Sama Vritti)

**What it is:** Equal-ratio breathing: inhale, hold, exhale, hold — all for the same count. "Sama" = equal; "Vritti" = movement.

**Technique:**
- Inhale for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Exhale for 4 counts
- Hold (empty) for 4 counts

**Advanced:** Extend the counts to 5, 6, or 8 as the practice develops.

**Benefits:** Powerful nervous system regulation. Used by military special forces and athletes for stress management. Activates the prefrontal cortex and reduces amygdala reactivity. Builds breath capacity.

**When to use:** Opening of class when the room feels agitated. As a standalone practice. Before meditation.

**Script:**
> "We'll try Box Breathing — four equal sides like a square. Inhale with me… 2, 3, 4. Hold… 2, 3, 4. Exhale… 2, 3, 4. Hold empty… 2, 3, 4. Again — inhale… Let the breath become steady, like the four sides of a house."

## 4-7-8 Breath (Relaxing Breath)

**What it is:** A technique developed by Dr. Andrew Weil based on ancient pranayama. The extended exhale activates the vagus nerve and parasympathetic nervous system.

**Technique:**
- Inhale through the nose for **4** counts
- Hold for **7** counts
- Exhale through the mouth for **8** counts (with a "whoosh" sound)

**When to use:** Cool-down, Savasana preparation, or any deeply restorative moment. Excellent for insomnia and high anxiety.

**Note:** Not suitable at the beginning of a heating Vinyasa class — it is profoundly calming.

## Kapalabhati (Skull-Shining Breath)

**What it is:** A vigorous, energizing cleansing breath. Short, forceful exhales through the nose (belly pumps in sharply), with passive inhales.

**Technique:** 
- Take a deep inhale. Begin sharp, rhythmic exhales through the nose (about 1 per second), contracting the belly sharply on each exhale. The inhale happens passively.
- Start with 20–30 pumps. Rest. Repeat 2–3 rounds.

**Benefits:** Clears the respiratory tract, builds heat and energy, stimulates the digestive system, awakens the mind.

**When to use:** Beginning of a heating practice — a wonderful energizer. NOT before rest or meditation (it's activating). NOT for pregnant women, those with high blood pressure, or respiratory issues.

**Script:**
> "We'll awaken the breath with Kapalabhati. Take a full inhale. Now — short, sharp exhales through the nose, belly pumping in. Inhale is passive — it takes care of itself. Let's begin: ha-ha-ha-ha-ha. Let the belly do the work. The exhale is the effort, the inhale is the gift."

---

## Sitali (Cooling Breath)

**What it is:** Breathing through a rolled or folded tongue (like a straw), which cools the air as it enters. The breath is then exhaled through the nose.

**Technique:**
- Roll the tongue into a tube (if you can; if not, breathe through gently parted teeth — Sitkari variation).
- Inhale slowly through the tongue/teeth, feeling the cool air.
- Close the mouth, exhale through the nose.
- Repeat 5–10 rounds.

**When to use:** Hot yoga, summer classes, any time the body or room is overheated.

**Script:**
> "Roll or fold the tongue, or part the lips slightly. Inhale through the tongue — feel how cool that air is. Now close the mouth and exhale through the nose. A few rounds of this brings the body temperature down beautifully."

## Breath Sequencing in Class

| Phase | Recommended Breath |
|-------|--------------------|
| Arriving / Opening | Natural observation → 3-Part Breath or Nadi Shodhana |
| Building heat | Ujjayi throughout |
| Peak poses | Ujjayi, strong and steady |
| Cool-down | Ease Ujjayi, return to natural breath |
| Final poses / Savasana prep | 4-7-8 or natural diaphragmatic |
| Savasana | Natural breathing, barely perceptible |
| Meditation closing | Natural breath or very subtle 3-Part |

---

## General Breath Cues (Use Throughout Class)

- "Inhale as you expand; exhale as you release."
- "Let the inhale create length; the exhale create depth."
- "If you've lost the breath, back out of the pose until you find it."
- "Every exhale is an opportunity to let something go."
- "The breath is your anchor. When the mind wanders, the breath brings you home."
- "You don't need to make the breath louder — just more conscious."
