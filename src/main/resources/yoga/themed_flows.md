# Themed Vinyasa Flows — Advanced Practice Variety

> A collection of themed flows for variety in advanced Vinyasa practice. Each theme shapes the sequencing, the tone, the pace, and the teaching language. Use these themes to build classes with a clear emotional and physical arc.

---

## Theme 1: Chandra Namaskar — Moon Salutation Flow (60 min)

**Theme:** Yin, lunar energy, receptivity, softening. The complement to the solar Sun Salutation.  
**Tone:** Slow, quiet, spacious. Long holds. "We are not building heat — we are finding what is already warm."  
**Timing:** Ideal for evening, full moon, or days when the body needs restoration alongside movement.  
**Energetic quality:** Cooling, introspective, Apana Vayu (downward energy)

### Moon Salutation Sequence (Chandra Namaskar)
A lateral, wide-leg flow — moves side to side rather than front to back.

| Step | Pose | German | Breath |
|------|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Tadasana (Mountain) | Bergpose | Neutral |
| 2 | Urdhva Hastasana | Arme hoch | Inhale |
| 3 | Uttanasana (Forward Fold) | Vorbeuge | Exhale |
| 4 | Ashta Chandrasana (Crescent Moon right) | Halbmond rechts | Inhale |
| 5 | Skandasana (Side Squat right) | Seitliche Hocke rechts | Exhale |
| 6 | Wide Squat / Goddess | Göttin | Inhale |
| 7 | Skandasana (Side Squat left) | Seitliche Hocke links | Exhale |
| 8 | Ashta Chandrasana (Crescent Moon left) | Halbmond links | Inhale |
| 9 | Uttanasana (Forward Fold) | Vorbeuge | Exhale |
| 10 | Urdhva Hastasana | Arme hoch | Inhale |
| 11 | Tadasana | Bergpose | Exhale |

**Teaching note:** The Moon Salutation does NOT include Chaturanga or Upward Dog. It is a lateral arc, not a forward progression. Repeat 6–8 rounds at a slow pace.

### Opening (5 min)
- **Viparita Karani** (Legs up the wall, 3 min): Arrive in stillness. Let the nervous system understand this is not a solar practice.
- **Supta Baddha Konasana** (2 min): Soles together, knees wide. "Let the hips be heavy."

### Moon Salutations (15 min)
- 6 rounds of Chandra Namaskar at a slow, meditative pace. "Let the lateral arc of the body mirror the arc of the moon."

### Lunar Standing Sequence (12 min)
- **Goddess Pose** (Utkata Konasana, 10 breaths): Wide stance, toes out, knees bent. Slow pulse × 5. "Root down. Feel the earth."
- **Triangle Pose** (Trikonasana) right and left (8 breaths each): "Lateral extension — the body makes a star shape."
- **Revolved Triangle** (Parivrtta Trikonasana) right and left (5 breaths each): Slow, deliberate twist.
- **Extended Side Angle** (Parsvakonasana) right and left (8 breaths each): Bottom forearm to thigh.

### Lunar Hip Sequence (15 min)
- **Baddha Konasana** (Bound Angle, 3 min): Forward fold over the soles. "No pushing — let the hips open in their own time."
- **Malasana** (Garland Squat, 2 min): Heels on the floor or a fold. "The earth holds you."
- **Yin Pigeon / Sleeping Swan** right (3 min): Completely passive. No muscular effort.
- **Yin Pigeon** left (3 min)

### Restorative Close (10 min)
- **Supta Matsyendrasana** (Supine Twist, 2 min each side)
- **Supta Baddha Konasana** (3 min) with optional eye pillow
- **Savasana** (5 min): Guided: "Let the body be held by the night. You don't need to do anything more."

## Theme 2: Tapas — Fire Flow (45 min)

**Theme:** Tapas (disciplined heat), burning off resistance, the transformative power of effort.  
**Tone:** Fierce but steady. Internal fire. "The flame does not rage — it is consistent and clear."  
**Apex:** Strong core, Navasana variations, Plank family, arm balance transition  
**Sanskrit intention:** "Tapas" — the Sanskrit for heat/discipline; one of the Niyamas (personal observances)

### Opening (3 min)
- **Kapalabhati Pranayama** (Skull-Shining Breath, 3 rounds × 30 pumps): "Light the internal fire."
- **Agni Sara** (Fire Wash, 3 rounds, optional): On empty exhale — pump the belly rapidly × 20. Resets the digestive fire.
- **Cat-Cow** × 8: "Build the spine before the flame reaches it."

### Sun Salutations — Fast Fire (10 min)
- **Sun Sal A × 6**: Rounds 1–2 slow. Rounds 3–6: one breath per movement. "Match the breath to the fire."
- **Sun Sal B × 4**: Add Utkatasana (Chair) hold for 10 breaths on round 4. "Let the thighs burn. Stay."

### Core Ignition (12 min)
- **Navasana** (Boat) × 5: Hold 10 breaths each. Between rounds — lower to a 2-inch hover (Paripurna Navasana lowering). "The heat is not the enemy — it is the teacher."
- **Plank** (10 breaths) → **Forearm Plank** (10 breaths) → **Side Plank right** (8 breaths) → **Forearm Plank** (5 breaths) → **Side Plank left** (8 breaths)
- **Hollow Body Hold** (10 breaths): Lower back pressed into the mat, legs and shoulders lifted. "Absolute stillness in the fire."
- **Plank → Knee-to-Elbow** pulses right × 10 → **Knee-to-Opposite-Elbow** × 10 → rest in Child's Pose. Left side × same.

### Standing Heat (10 min)
Right side → Vinyasa → Left side.
- **Warrior III** (10 breaths): "Hips level. One strong line."
- **Standing Split** (5 breaths): Forward fold, reach both hands to the floor.
- **Warrior I** (8 breaths) → **Chair Pose** (10 breaths): "Sink lower. The legs can handle it."
- **Eagle Pose** (Garudasana, 8 breaths each side): Double the wrap — thighs squeeze like a vice.

### Fire Peak — Arm Balance (8 min)
- **Crow (Bakasana)** × 3 rounds: Each round, stay one breath longer than comfortable. "One more breath — that is where the transformation lives."
- **Transition Bakasana → Chaturanga** (float back): "Empty the lungs, draw the belly in, fly back."
- **Child's Pose** (5 breaths)

### Cool-Down (5 min)
- **Paschimottanasana** (2 min): "The flame becomes warmth."
- **Supine Twist** (1 min each side)
- **Savasana** (5 min): "The fire has done its work. Now rest in the warmth it left behind."

## Theme 3: Shodana — Twist & Detox Flow (45 min)

**Theme:** Shodana means purification. Twisting compresses and releases organs, increases spinal mobility, and symbolically wrings out what no longer serves.  
**Tone:** Wringing, cleansing, releasing. "With each exhale — let go of what you no longer need."  
**Physical focus:** Thoracic rotation, digestive organ massage, spinal decompression

### Opening (4 min)
- **Seated Meditation** (1 min): Arrive. Notice if there's tension along the spine.
- **Seated Spinal Twist** (Ardha Matsyendrasana prep): Gentle seated twist to right and left, 5 breaths each. "The spine wants to rotate — let it lead."
- **Reclined Knees-to-Chest** (1 min): Rock side to side.

### Sun Salutations — Twist Emphasis (10 min)
- **Sun Sal A × 3**: In each Down Dog → add a 3-legged dog with open hip, knee spiraling up.
- **Sun Sal B × 3**: After each Warrior I → add Parivrtta Utkatasana (Twisted Chair) before the Vinyasa. "The class is already twisting — let the salutations reflect that."

### Standing Twist Sequence (15 min)
Right side → Vinyasa → Left side.
- **Warrior I** (5 breaths) → **Parivrtta Parsvakonasana** (Revolved Side Angle, 8 breaths): Elbow outside the front knee, twist. "The front knee drives the twist — press the knee into the arm."
- **Parivrtta Trikonasana** (Revolved Triangle, 8 breaths): "This is the deepest standing twist. Long spine first — then rotate."
- **Parivritta Ardha Chandrasana** (Revolved Half Moon, 5 breaths): One hand on a block below the shoulder, top arm reaches up. Balance + twist simultaneously. "Soft gaze. Strong center."
- **Vinyasa**

### Deep Twist Sequence (10 min)
- **Parivrtta Janu Sirsasana** (Revolved Head-to-Knee, 2 min each side): Side stretch + twist. Reach the top arm over the head.
- **Ardha Matsyendrasana** (Half Lord of the Fishes, 2 min each side): "Sit tall first. Then twist from the base. The spine must be long before it can rotate."
- **Pasasana** (Noose Pose, 1 min each side, if available): Deep squat + full bind twist. "This is the maximum thoracic rotation — only go as far as the breath stays even."

### Floor Finish (6 min)
- **Jathara Parivartanasana** (Reclined Twist, 1.5 min each side): Both knees to one side. "Complete the wringing."
- **Apanasana** (Knees-to-Chest, 1 min): Rock gently. Stimulate the apana — the downward-cleansing energy.
- **Savasana** (5 min): "The body is washed clean. Rest in that clarity."

## Theme 4: Svadhisthana — Chakra Flow (75 min)

**Theme:** Moving upward through the seven chakras — from root to crown. Each section embodies one chakra's quality.  
**Tone:** Begins heavy and earthy, progressively becomes lighter and more expansive.  
**Structure:** 7 sections of ~7–10 minutes each. Can be split into shorter classes per chakra.

### Muladhara — Root Chakra (Earth, Stability) — 8 min
Color: Red. Element: Earth. Location: Base of spine.
- **Tadasana** (2 min): Stand in stillness. "Feel the soles of the feet. You belong to the earth."
- **Warrior II** × 3 (8 breaths each): Strong stance, grounded back foot. "You are a tree — the roots go deep."
- **Malasana** (2 min): Low squat, toes wide. "Return to the earth. Let it hold you."
- *Cue:* "With each exhale, release into the ground. Trust that it will hold."

### Svadhisthana — Sacral Chakra (Water, Creativity) — 8 min
Color: Orange. Element: Water. Location: Sacrum/pelvis.
- **Goddess Pose** (Utkata Konasana) with slow hip circles × 10 each direction. "The pelvis is the seat of creativity — let it move freely."
- **Butterfly / Baddha Konasana** (2 min): Slow knee pulses, then hold. "Soften the groin."
- **Lizard Pose** right and left (1 min each): "The hips hold the stories. Let them open."
- *Cue:* "Flow like water — no rigidity. Find the fluid quality of movement."

### Manipura — Solar Plexus Chakra (Fire, Power) — 10 min
Color: Yellow. Element: Fire. Location: Navel center.
- **Navasana × 3** (8 breaths each): "Your personal power lives here. Stoke it."
- **Plank** (5 breaths) → **Chaturanga hover** (5 breaths): "The core is the furnace."
- **Sun Sal A × 2**: "Move with the fire you've just built."
- *Cue:* "This is your Manipura — your will, your discipline, your fire. Let it burn cleanly."

### Anahata — Heart Chakra (Air, Love) — 10 min
Color: Green. Element: Air. Location: Heart center.
- **Ustrasana × 2** (8 breaths each): "The backbend asks you to open what you protect most."
- **Wild Thing** × 2 each side (5 breaths): "Let the heart tip toward the sky — be surprised by your own openness."
- **Bhujangasana** (Full Cobra, 5 breaths): "The cobra rises. The heart follows."
- **Anahatasana** (Melting Heart, 2 min): "Melt. The floor is trustworthy. The heart is safe."
- *Cue:* "What would it feel like to open this completely? This is the invitation — not the demand."

### Vishuddha — Throat Chakra (Sound, Truth) — 8 min
Color: Blue. Element: Ether/Space. Location: Throat.
- **Matsyasana** (Fish Pose, 1 min): "The throat opens. The voice is free."
- **Setu Bandhasana** (Bridge, 3 rounds): Chin drops slightly — neck extension opens the throat.
- **Shoulderstand** (Salamba Sarvangasana, 3 min, if accessible): "The throat receives the weight of the world — gently, with support."
- *Cue:* "Breathe into the throat. Make sound if you are moved to — a hum, a sigh, anything authentic."

### Ajna — Third Eye Chakra (Intuition, Vision) — 10 min
Color: Indigo. Element: Light. Location: Brow center.
- **Balasana** (Child's Pose, 2 min): Forehead to the floor. "The third eye connects with the earth."
- **Sirsasana** (Headstand, if established) or Dolphin (3–5 min): "Inversions shift perspective. Let go of the conditioned view."
- **Trataka-inspired Drishti work**: In three poses of your choice — hold a steady, unblinking gaze. Notice what sharpens when the eyes are still.
- *Cue:* "Close the eyes. See inward. What do you know — beyond what you've been taught?"

### Sahasrara — Crown Chakra (Consciousness, Unity) — 8 min
Color: Violet/White. Element: Thought. Location: Crown.
- **Viparita Karani** (Legs up the wall, 3 min): "The crown points toward the floor — humility, surrender."
- **Savasana** (5 min, fully guided): "You are not the body, not the thoughts, not the practice. You are the awareness that witnesses all of this. Rest in that."
- *Cue:* "There is nothing to do here. The practice has dissolved. Only presence remains."

## Theme 5: Prana Vayu — Shoulder & Upper Body Opening Flow (60 min)

**Theme:** Freeing the upper body — thoracic spine, shoulders, chest, wrists. The gateway to inversions and arm balances.  
**Tone:** Technical and precise, with warmth. "The shoulders carry everything — today we put it down."  
**Apex:** Gomukhasana arms (deep shoulder opener), Dolphin, Pincha Mayurasana preparation

### Opening (5 min)
- **Wrist warm-up** (2 min): Circles, extension, flexion, weight-shifting in Tabletop.
- **Thread the Needle** (1 min each side): Shoulder internal rotation release.
- **Puppy Pose** (Anahatasana, 2 min): Arms forward, hips over knees, chest drops. "The thoracic spine is the target — not the lower back."

### Sun Salutations — Shoulder Focus (12 min)
- **Sun Sal A × 5**: Hold each Down Dog for 8 breaths (instead of 5). "Actively press the floor away. Feel the serratus anterior — the 'wings' under the armpits — engage."
- Between rounds 3 and 4: **Puppy Pose** (1 min).

### Thoracic & Shoulder Sequence (15 min)
- **Garudasana (Eagle) arms** × 3 (8 breaths each): Alternate top arm. Feel the space between the shoulder blades. "This is the thoracic spine breathing."
- **Paschima Namaskar** (Reverse Prayer, 3 rounds × 5 breaths): Hands in prayer behind the back. Shoulders back and down.
- **Gomukhasana (Cow Face) arms** (2 min each side): Top elbow toward ceiling, bottom elbow toward floor. Bind or use a strap. "The shoulder rotators are getting full range here — be patient."
- **Parivrtta Parsvakonasana with shoulder opener** (5 breaths each side): Bottom arm through under the front thigh, bind if available.
- **Dolphin** (1 min × 3 rounds): "Press the forearms actively — this is the foundation of Forearm Stand."

### Inversion Preparation (15 min)
- **L-shape at wall** (1 min × 3): Hips directly above wrists. "Feel vertical without being vertical — the body learns the line."
- **Pincha Mayurasana at wall** (10 breaths × 3): Focus on the elbows staying shoulder-width. "The elbows are the pillars. They must not fall."
- **Free exploration of Pincha Mayurasana** (for those ready)

### Chest Opening Cool-Down (8 min)
- **Supta Matsyasana** (Reclined Fish, 2 min): Bolster along the spine, arms wide. "The chest opens. The shoulders drop away from the ears."
- **Supta Gomukhasana** (Supine Cow Face arms, 1 min each side)
- **Savasana** (5 min): "Every tension the upper body has held — let the floor receive it."

## Theme 6: Yin-Yang Balance Flow (75 min)

**Theme:** The polarity of effort and surrender. First half is dynamic Vinyasa (Yang); second half is long-hold Yin.  
**Tone:** First half: energetic, clear, purposeful. Second half: silent, yielding, patient.  
**Transition cue:** "We have built the fire. Now we sit in the warmth. The body will open differently now that it is warm."

### Yang Phase — Power Vinyasa (40 min)

**Opening** (4 min): Kapalabhati × 2 rounds. Cat-Cow × 10. Down Dog (5 breaths).

**Sun Salutations** (12 min): Sun Sal A × 5, Sun Sal B × 4. Full pace from round 2.

**Standing Peak** (15 min), right then left:
- **Warrior II → Reverse Warrior → Extended Side Angle** (flow, 2 breaths each, × 3 rounds)
- **Warrior III → Half Moon** (Ardha Chandrasana, 5 breaths each)
- **Vinyasa**
- **Chair → Eagle** (Garudasana, 8 breaths) × 2

**Core** (5 min):
- **Navasana × 3** (8 breaths)
- **Side Plank** × 2 each side

**Bridge/Wheel** (4 min): Bridge × 2, Wheel × 1 (if available).

### Transition (2 min)
- Come to the floor. Lie on the back. "We shift from Yang to Yin. The effort is complete. What remains is surrender."
- 10 deep breaths. Nowhere to go.

### Yin Phase — Deep Hold (30 min)

All poses held 3–5 minutes. No muscular effort — find the bone-and-connective-tissue edge, not the muscle edge.

- **Supta Baddha Konasana** (4 min): "Let the weight of the legs open the hips."
- **Yin Pigeon right** (4 min): "The muscle work is done. Now let the connective tissue respond."
- **Yin Pigeon left** (4 min)
- **Caterpillar** (Paschimottanasana Yin, 4 min): Rounded spine is fine. "The spine decompresses from below."
- **Dragon** (Low Lunge Yin, 3 min each side): Knee down, sinking into the hip flexor. "The psoas is whispering — listen."
- **Supine Twist right** (3 min) → **Supine Twist left** (3 min)
- **Savasana** (7 min): "You have been both the fire and the still point. Rest in both."

## Theme 7: Apana — Grounding & Earth Flow (45 min)

**Theme:** Muladhara chakra, earth element, Apana Vayu (downward energy). The antidote to scattered, anxious energy.  
**Tone:** Heavy, rooted, stable. Slow exhales. "The earth is not asking you to do anything — only to arrive."  
**Ideal for:** Days of overwhelm, anxiety, mental scatter, or high wind/Vata energy

### Opening (5 min)
- **Standing Tadasana** (2 min): Eyes closed, feet hip-width. "Feel every toe. Press them into the floor. Start from the ground up."
- **Slow forward fold from standing** (Uttanasana, 3 min): Completely heavy, head hangs, arms dangle. "Gravity is not a threat — it is a welcome home."

### Sun Salutations — Grounding Pace (10 min)
- **Sun Sal A × 3**: Deliberately slow. Every Down Dog held for 10 breaths. Heel-press to the floor emphasized. "Root down to rise."

### Lower Body Rooting Sequence (15 min)
- **Warrior II right** (10 breaths): Heavy back foot. "The back foot is the anchor of the whole ship."
- **Extended Side Angle** right (8 breaths): Forearm on thigh, heavy.
- **Parsvakonasana bind** right (if available, 5 breaths)
- **Prasarita Padottanasana** (Wide-Leg Forward Fold, 3 min): Head heavy toward the floor. "The whole spine decompresses."
- All above, left side.

### Earth Floor Sequence (10 min)
- **Balasana** (2 min): "Forehead to earth. The most ancient posture — the body knows this shape."
- **Janu Sirsasana** (1.5 min each side): Grounded hip, heavy forward fold.
- **Suptam Baddha Konasana** (3 min): Bolster along the spine or flat. "Spread like a starfish. Nothing to hold."

### Savasana (7 min)
Long, guided: "You are made of the same elements as the earth — carbon, water, mineral. When you lie here, you are returning what was borrowed. The body is held completely. You don't need to hold anything."

## Theme 8: Morning Sun — Energizing Wake-Up Flow (30 min)

**Theme:** Activating the body and mind after sleep. Surya energy, prana awakening.  
**Tone:** Bright, uplifting. "This practice lights the lamp — let it burn all day."  
**Ideal for:** Early morning, before work, when time is short but quality is non-negotiable

### Opening (2 min)
- **Kapalabhati** × 2 rounds (30 pumps each): Immediately activating.
- **Arms overhead, big inhale → exhale sigh** × 3: Wake up the respiratory system.

### Sun Salutations — The Full Dose (18 min)
- **Sun Sal A × 5**: Each round builds pace. Round 5: jump-back, jump-forward.
- **Sun Sal B × 5**: With full Warrior I holds (5 breaths). On round 5 — add a Warrior II and Reverse Warrior before Vinyasa.

### Quick Standing Sequence (5 min)
- **Chair Pose** (5 breaths) → **Eagle Pose** right and left (5 breaths each): Balance + strength to close.
- **Dancer Pose** (Natarajasana, 3 breaths each side): "Open the heart toward the morning."

### Brief Close (5 min)
- **Paschimottanasana** (1 min)
- **Supine Twist** (1 min each side)
- **Savasana** (2 min): "Short but complete. The body has been activated. Carry this energy forward."

## Theme 9: Ahimsa — Emotional Release & Heart Opening (60 min)

**Theme:** Ahimsa (non-violence) toward the self. Moving through places of holding — hips, chest, throat — with compassion.  
**Tone:** Tender, spacious, permission-giving. "There is nothing to fix here. Only something to feel."  
**Physical focus:** Hip flexors, heart center, inner groin — the places where emotion physically lives

### Opening (8 min)
- **Supta Baddha Konasana** (3 min): "Arrive exactly as you are. No performance today."
- **Reclined Hamstring stretch** (Supta Padangusthasana I, 1 min each side): Let the leg be heavy.
- **Knees-to-chest** (1 min): Rock gently. "This is home."
- **Cat-Cow** (3 min slow): Let the movement be emotional as well as physical.

### Sun Salutations — Soft (8 min)
- **Sun Sal A × 3**: Cobra (not Up Dog). Step back (not jump). "This is not a performance — it is an offering."

### Hip & Heart Opening Sequence (20 min)
- **Anjaneyasana** (Low Lunge) right (2 min): Arms wide, chest open. "Breathe into the front of the hip. Let it soften."
- **Lizard right** (2 min): Forearms down. "The hip may resist — meet that resistance with curiosity, not force."
- **Pigeon right** (3 min): Walk hands forward, rest forehead. "If anything arises — let it. The hips store our stories."
- Transition to Down Dog, left side × same.
- **Ustrasana** (Camel, 2 rounds × 8 breaths): "The backbend asks for courage. Open the chest — open the heart."
- **Anahatasana** (Melting Heart, 2 min): "Surrender the chest to gravity. You are held."

### Surrender Sequence (10 min)
- **Upavistha Konasana** (Wide-Angle Forward Fold, 2 min): "The effort is behind you. This is the giving over."
- **Paschimottanasana** (2 min)
- **Supta Matsyendrasana** (1.5 min each side): "Let the twist wring out what remains."
- **Happy Baby** (1 min): Rock gently. "This is where we began — and where we return."

### Savasana (10 min)
Long, slow, fully guided: "You practiced Ahimsa today — non-violence toward yourself. You met the edges with kindness. You breathed through what was difficult. You gave the body permission to feel. Now rest in that care. You are enough, exactly as you are. This practice was enough. This moment is enough."

## Theme 10: Ashtanga Primary-Inspired Standing Sequence (45 min)

**Theme:** The discipline of a traditional form. Ashtanga's Primary Series (Yoga Chikitsa — yoga therapy) has a fixed standing sequence that builds systematically. Adapted here for Vinyasa pacing.  
**Tone:** Disciplined, focused, methodical. "The sequence is the teacher."  
**Note:** This is the Ashtanga standing sequence without the full Primary seated series. Ideal as a standalone class.

### Opening (3 min)
- **Samasthiti** (standing meditation, 2 min): Eyes open, soft gaze. "The practice begins in stillness."
- **Mangala Mantra** (optional): Om × 3 or Ashtanga opening prayer (silent or aloud).

### Primary Standing Sequence (35 min)
Each pose vinyasa-linked. Right side then left. Traditional Ashtanga breath count maintained.

1. **Padangusthasana** (Big Toe Pose, 5 breaths): Feet hip-width, fold, grip big toes.
2. **Padahastasana** (Hand-Under-Foot Pose, 5 breaths): Hands under feet, fold deeper.
3. **Utthita Trikonasana** (Extended Triangle, 5 breaths each side)
4. **Parivrtta Trikonasana** (Revolved Triangle, 5 breaths each side)
5. **Utthita Parsvakonasana** (Extended Side Angle, 5 breaths each side)
6. **Parivrtta Parsvakonasana** (Revolved Side Angle, 5 breaths each side)
7. **Prasarita Padottanasana A** (Wide-Leg Forward Fold, head down, 5 breaths)
8. **Prasarita Padottanasana B** (hands on hips, 5 breaths)
9. **Prasarita Padottanasana C** (arms interlaced behind, 5 breaths)
10. **Prasarita Padottanasana D** (grip big toes, 5 breaths)
11. **Parsvottanasana** (Pyramid Pose, 5 breaths each side)
12. **Utthita Hasta Padangusthasana A** (Standing Hand to Big Toe, front, 5 breaths each side)
13. **Utthita Hasta Padangusthasana B** (leg to side, 5 breaths each side)
14. **Ardha Baddha Padmottanasana** (Half Bound Lotus Forward Fold, 5 breaths each side)
15. **Utkatasana** (Chair, 5 breaths)
16. **Virabhadrasana I** (Warrior I, 5 breaths each side)
17. **Virabhadrasana II** (Warrior II, 5 breaths each side)

**Teaching note:** In Ashtanga tradition, the teacher does NOT describe every pose — students know the sequence. Call the name and the count. Trust the practitioner.

### Close (7 min)
- **Dandasana** (Staff Pose, 3 breaths): "The standing sequence is complete. Arrive seated."
- **Paschimottanasana** (2 min): The traditional counter-pose to the entire standing sequence.
- **Savasana** (5 min)
