# Guided Meditation Scripts

These are full scripts designed to be spoken aloud as the closing meditation of a yoga class. Each includes timing, tone guidance, and pause indicators.

**Delivery notes:**
- Speak slowly — slower than feels natural.
- Use [pause] markers — these are actual silences (5–10 seconds).
- Let your voice become softer and lower as the meditation deepens.
- A good meditation voice is warm, steady, unhurried.

---

## 1. Body Scan (4–5 minutes)

**Best for:** Releasing physical tension. Works beautifully at the end of any class.  
**Setup:** Students in Savasana, eyes closed.

---

*"Take a few natural breaths and let the body sink into the mat. You've done the work — this is the receiving.*

*We'll begin a slow journey through the body. Wherever I bring your attention, simply notice what's there — no need to change anything.*

[pause]

*Bring your awareness to the soles of the feet. Notice any sensation here — warmth, tingling, weight. Let the feet soften.*

[pause]

*Move up to the calves and shins. Notice. Let them release.*

[pause]

*The knees. The thighs. Let the legs be heavy — completely supported by the earth beneath you.*

[pause]

*Bring your attention to the hips and pelvis. This is often where we store a great deal. Notice anything that's here — and gently invite it to soften on the exhale.*

[pause]

*The belly. Feel the gentle rise and fall of the breath. The digestive organs settling. The whole center of the body relaxing.*

[pause]

*Move up to the chest. The heart space. Notice the heartbeat — faithful and steady, beating for you without any effort on your part. Let the chest open and soften.*

[pause]

*The hands. The fingers. Let them uncurl. The forearms, the upper arms. The shoulders — often the last place we let go. Imagine the shoulders becoming heavy as stones, sinking into the floor.*

[pause]

*The neck. The jaw. The jaw is often a repository of everything we didn't say, didn't release. Let it hang loose. The tongue falls away from the roof of the mouth.*

[pause]

*The eyes — soft behind the lids. The brow smooth. The whole face like a still lake at dawn.*

[pause]

*Now bring your awareness to the whole body at once — from the soles of the feet to the crown of the head. One unified, breathing, living being. Completely at rest.*

[long pause — 30 seconds]

*Begin to deepen the breath. Let the body know that rest is complete. Gently wiggle the fingers and toes. When you're ready, roll to one side. Take a moment there. And slowly — in your own time — return to sitting."*

## 2. Breath Awareness (3–4 minutes)

**Best for:** Calming a scattered mind. Simple, powerful, accessible to all.  
**Setup:** Savasana or seated.

---

*"Settle into stillness. Close your eyes.*

*Bring all of your attention to the breath. Not changing it — just noticing it.*

[pause]

*Where do you feel the breath most vividly right now? Perhaps the nostrils — the cool air entering, slightly warm as it leaves. Perhaps the chest, rising gently. The belly, lifting and falling.*

*Choose one place and rest your attention there.*

[pause]

*The breath has a beginning — the first moment of the inhale. A middle — the full expansion. An end — the last whisper of the exhale. And then a space… a natural pause before the next breath is born.*

*Follow this cycle. Beginning, middle, end, space.*

[pause]

*When the mind wanders — and it will, because that's what minds do — simply notice that it has gone, and gently bring it back to the breath. No frustration. Every return is the practice.*

[pause]

*The breath is always here. Always in this moment. When you follow it, you are here too.*

[pause — 30 seconds]

*Let the breath return to its natural rhythm. Begin to notice the sounds around you — near, then far. The weight of the body against the floor. And when you are ready, let the eyes gently open.*"

## 3. Loving-Kindness Meditation (Metta, 5–6 minutes)

**Best for:** Closing a class with a theme of compassion, self-acceptance, or community.  
**Setup:** Seated in Sukhasana (easy cross-legged) or Savasana.

---

*"Come to a comfortable position. Close your eyes. Place one hand on the heart if that feels right.*

*Take a few breaths to arrive.*

[pause]

*We'll practice Metta — loving-kindness. It's simple and ancient. We'll direct warm wishes, first to ourselves, then outward in expanding circles.*

*Begin with yourself. Bring your own image to mind — not how you wish you were, but exactly as you are, right now, in this moment. With all your imperfections, your struggles, your tenderness.*

*And silently, or whispered inwardly, offer these words to yourself:*

*May I be happy.*
*[pause]*
*May I be healthy.*
*[pause]*
*May I be safe.*
*[pause]*
*May I live with ease.*

[pause]

*Let those words land. Notice any resistance — perhaps it feels strange to wish yourself well. If so, that's okay. Just try it anyway.*

[pause]

*Now bring to mind someone you love easily — a close friend, a family member, perhaps an animal. Someone whose happiness brings you joy. See their face.*

*Offer the same words to them:*

*May you be happy.*
*May you be healthy.*
*May you be safe.*
*May you live with ease.*

[pause]

*Now expand the circle. Bring to mind someone neutral — someone you see but don't know well. A neighbor, a shopkeeper. Offer them the same wishes.*

*May you be happy. May you be healthy. May you be safe. May you live with ease.*

[pause]

*Let the circle expand now — to everyone in this room. We've shared this practice together today. May everyone here be happy, healthy, safe, and at ease.*

[pause]

*And finally — let the circle expand to all living beings everywhere. Every human, animal, creature. All of us navigating this strange, beautiful life.*

*May all beings be happy.*
*May all beings be healthy.*
*May all beings be safe.*
*May all beings live with ease.*

[pause — 20 seconds]

*Return now to yourself. How does the heart feel? Take a moment to rest here.*

*Slowly, let the eyes open. Let the light in gently.*"

## 4. Forest Visualization (4–5 minutes)

**Best for:** Grounding, stress relief, reconnecting with nature. Vivid and sensory.  
**Setup:** Savasana.

---

*"Let the body be completely still. Eyes closed. Let the weight of the day finally be set down.*

*With the next breath, I invite you to step — in your imagination — into a forest.*

[pause]

*It's a cool, quiet morning. The trees are tall — ancient, wise, unhurried. Light filters through the canopy in golden threads, shifting gently in a soft breeze.*

*Feel the ground beneath your feet. Rich, dark earth. Soft with moss and fallen leaves. Your footsteps are silent here.*

[pause]

*Take a breath and smell the air. Clean. Moist. Alive with the scent of pine, of bark, of earth after rain.*

[pause]

*You find a place to sit — at the base of a great old tree. Its roots rise and curve around you, like a gentle embrace. You lean back against the bark. You are held.*

[pause]

*Listen. Beneath the silence, there is sound. A bird in the distance. The wind moving through leaves far above. The creak of a branch. The forest breathes.*

[pause]

*You have no place to be. Nothing to do. Nowhere to get to. This moment is complete.*

*Sit here in the forest for a few breaths. Let the trees hold you.*

[pause — 30 seconds]

*When you're ready to return, take one more deep breath of forest air. Let it fill the lungs completely.*

*And on the exhale, begin to return — awareness of the mat beneath you, the room around you, the body breathing.*

*Wiggle the fingers and toes. Roll gently to one side. And slowly return."*

## 5. Yoga Nidra Introduction (7–10 minutes)

**Best for:** Deep rest, integration, end of an intense practice. Combines visualization with body awareness and sankalpa.  
**Setup:** Savasana with props as needed (blanket, eye pillow, support under knees).

---

*"Make any final adjustments to your Savasana. You will remain completely still for the next several minutes. If the body needs to move, let it — otherwise, allow stillness to deepen.*

*Yoga Nidra — yogic sleep — is a state between waking and sleep. You'll hear my voice and remain aware, while the body and mind enter deep rest. It's said that 30 minutes of Yoga Nidra equals 2–3 hours of sleep in terms of restorative benefit.*

*Take three deep, full breaths. Let each exhale release you a little more completely.*

[pause]

*Sankalpa — intention. Bring to mind a short, positive statement of who you are or what you're cultivating. Something like: 'I am at peace.' 'I am strong and open.' 'I choose joy.' State it internally three times, as if planting a seed.*

[pause]

*We'll rotate awareness through the body. As I name each part, simply bring your attention there — briefly, without effort.*

*Right hand thumb — index finger — middle finger — ring finger — little finger — palm — back of the hand — right wrist — forearm — elbow — upper arm — right shoulder — right armpit — right side of the chest — right side of the waist — right hip — right thigh — kneecap — back of the knee — calf — ankle — heel — sole of the right foot — right big toe — second toe — third — fourth — fifth.*

[pause]

*Left hand thumb — index finger — middle finger — ring finger — little finger — palm — back of the hand — left wrist — forearm — elbow — upper arm — left shoulder — left armpit — left side of the chest — left side of the waist — left hip — left thigh — kneecap — back of the knee — calf — ankle — heel — sole of the left foot — left big toe — second — third — fourth — fifth.*

[pause]

*The top of the head. The forehead. Right eyebrow — left eyebrow — the space between the eyebrows. Right eye — left eye. Right ear — left ear. Right cheek — left cheek. The nose. The upper lip. The lower lip. The chin. The throat. The whole face, completely relaxed.*

[pause]

*The whole body at once. A complete, breathing, luminous being. Resting.*

[long pause — 60 seconds of silence]

*Return now to your Sankalpa. State it internally three times — with feeling, as if it is already true.*

[pause]

*Begin to become aware of your breath. The body's weight against the mat. The sounds in the room. Without rushing, begin to bring small movements back — fingers, toes. Stretch the arms overhead. Take a full, yawning breath.*

*Roll to your right side and rest there for a moment. When you are ready, slowly — taking your time — return to sitting.*

*Welcome back."*

## Quick Closing Scripts (1–2 minutes)

For shorter classes or when full meditation is not possible.

**Brief Savasana send-off:**
> "Let the body be completely still. Let the breath soften. For the next few minutes, simply exist — not as a practitioner, not as a student, not as a role you play in the world. Just as awareness itself, resting. [pause] The practice is complete. The integration has begun."

**Gratitude close:**
> "Before we return to the world, take a moment of gratitude — for this body that carried you through the practice today. For the breath, always available. For this space, and for the people in it. [pause] From my heart to yours, Namasté."
