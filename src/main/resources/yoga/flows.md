# Yoga Flows Reference

> **Context:** These flows are designed for advanced Vinyasa practitioners with multiple years of experience. Assume the student knows Sun Salutations, Warriors, and foundational poses without instruction. Cues should be internal, minimal, and breath-focused. Offer depth variations, not modifications.

---

## Sun Salutation A (Surya Namaskar A)

The foundation of Vinyasa yoga. Typically 5–10 rounds to warm up the body.

| Step | Pose | German | Breath |
|------|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Tadasana (Mountain) | Bergpose | Neutral |
| 2 | Urdhva Hastasana (Arms Up) | Arme hoch | Inhale |
| 3 | Uttanasana (Forward Fold) | Vorbeuge | Exhale |
| 4 | Ardha Uttanasana (Halfway Lift) | Halbe Vorbeuge | Inhale |
| 5 | Chaturanga Dandasana (Low Push-Up) | Vier-Glieder-Stab | Exhale |
| 6 | Urdhva Mukha Svanasana (Upward Dog) | Aufwärtsschauender Hund | Inhale |
| 7 | Adho Mukha Svanasana (Down Dog) | Herabschauender Hund | Exhale (hold 5 breaths) |
| 8 | Ardha Uttanasana (Halfway Lift) | Halbe Vorbeuge | Inhale |
| 9 | Uttanasana (Forward Fold) | Vorbeuge | Exhale |
| 10 | Urdhva Hastasana (Arms Up) | Arme hoch | Inhale |
| 11 | Tadasana (Mountain) | Bergpose | Exhale |

**Teaching note:** On the first few rounds, offer modifications freely. Beginners can step back to plank instead of jumping, and use Cobra instead of Upward Dog.

## Advanced Flow 1: Power Vinyasa — Arm Balance Focus (60 minutes)

**Theme:** Building heat through strong transitions; arriving at Bakasana and Side Crow as peak.  
**Tone:** Direct, energetic, internal. Call the poses — trust they know them.  
**Apex:** Parsva Bakasana (Side Crow), Eka Pada Koundinyasana II (optional)

### Opening (4 min)
- **Sukhasana, eyes closed** (90 sec): Arrive in stillness. Notice the quality of the breath — not changing it yet.
- **Ujjayi breath introduction** (30 sec): "Narrow the back of the throat. Create the ocean sound. This breath carries you through the entire practice."
- **Cat-Cow** × 10 rounds: Add lateral movement — shift the hips in circles. Feel the full range of the spine.
- **Downward Dog** (5 breaths): Walk the heels, pedal, take inventory. "Notice what's here today without judgment."

### Sun Salutations (12 min)
- **Sun Sal A × 5**: First round slow. Rounds 2–5 build pace. From round 3: jump-back or float-back option. "Let the breath set the rhythm — not the other way around."
- **Sun Sal B × 4**: Full Warrior I. From round 2: add a breath of Low Lunge before Warrior I. On round 4: hold Warrior I for 5 breaths — feel the hip flexor of the back leg lengthen completely.

### Standing Twist Preparation (8 min)
Building the thoracic rotation needed for Side Crow.
- **Parivrtta Utkatasana (Twisted Chair)** right × 2 (5 breaths each): "Rotate from the ribs — not the shoulders, not the lower back. The thoracic spine is where the twist lives."
- **Vinyasa**
- **Parivrtta Utkatasana** left × 2 (5 breaths each)
- **Parivrtta Parsvakonasana (Twisted Side Angle)** right (5 breaths): "Drive the back heel down. The twist deepens on every exhale."
- **Vinyasa** → **Parivrtta Parsvakonasana** left
- **Down Dog** (5 breaths)

### Core & Hip Flexor Sequence (6 min)
Building Uddiyana bandha for arm balance entry.
- **Navasana × 3** (8 breaths each): Between rounds — lower to a hover, hold 3 breaths (Lolasana prep). "The belly supports everything. Find your root here."
- **Plank** (5 breaths) → **Side Plank right** (Vasisthasana, 5 breaths) → **Plank** (3 breaths) → **Side Plank left** (5 breaths)
- **Down Dog**

### Arm Balance Peak (12 min)
- **Malasana** (Garland, 8 breaths): Heels on floor or on a fold. Elbows press the knees wide. "Soften the hip joints. Let gravity do the opening."
- **Bakasana (Crow) — exploration** (3 rounds, 5–8 breaths each): Between rounds, rest in Malasana. "Notice which round feels most easeful. That's your bandha working."
- **Parsva Bakasana (Side Crow)** right: From Malasana → deep twist to the right → hands shoulder-width to the right → knees onto the left upper arm. "Both knees stack. Hips over hands." 3–5 breaths.
- **Vinyasa**
- **Parsva Bakasana** left. 3–5 breaths.
- *Optional depth:* **Eka Pada Koundinyasana II** right → left (from Lizard, for those ready)
- **Child's Pose** (10 breaths): Complete rest.

### Hip Openers & Cool-Down (12 min)
- **Pigeon right** (2 min): "Let this be an arrival, not a push. The work is done — now you soften."
- **Down Dog** → **Pigeon left** (2 min)
- **Paschimottanasana** (Seated Forward Fold, 2 min): Spine long, fold from the hips. "On each exhale — a little more release."
- **Supta Matsyendrasana** (Supine Twist) right (1 min) → left (1 min)
- **Setu Bandhasana** (Bridge) × 2 (5 breaths): Counter-pose for the hip flexor work.

### Savasana (6 min)
"Lower down completely. Release the effort of the practice from every cell. The body knows what to do now — let it integrate."

## Advanced Flow 2: Inversion Deep Dive (75 minutes)

**Theme:** Going upside down as a practice of trust and perspective.  
**Tone:** Grounded, patient, technical. Precise cues for shoulder mechanics.  
**Apex:** Handstand (free-standing or wall), Pincha Mayurasana

### Opening (5 min)
- **Virasana (Hero Pose)** or Sukhasana (2 min): "Feel the weight of the sit-bones. This is ground. We return to this no matter how high we go."
- **Neck rolls** (slow, 3 each direction)
- **Shoulder circles** (10 large circles each direction): Feel the full range of the shoulder joint.
- **Eagle arms** × 2 (5 breaths each, alternating top arm): Thoracic opening.
- **Cat-Cow with shoulder rolls** × 8: On Cow — retract the shoulder blades. On Cat — protract fully. "This is the movement the Handstand needs."

### Sun Salutations — Shoulder Focus (12 min)
- **Sun Sal A × 4**: Hold Plank for 5 breaths (instead of going to Chaturanga) in rounds 2 and 3. "Active shoulders — push the floor away. Feel the serratus engage."
- **Sun Sal B × 3**: On the Down Dog holds — pedal heels, then take both heels to the floor simultaneously. Press actively through the fingers.

### Dolphin Sequence — Inversion Foundation (10 min)
The Dolphin is the backbone of all forearm-based inversions.
- **Dolphin** (Ardha Pincha Mayurasana) × 5 holds (8 breaths each): Between holds — rest in Child's Pose. Elbows shoulder-width, forearms flat, press actively. "The forearms are a shelf you build, not a surface you collapse into."
- **Dolphin walks**: From Dolphin, slowly walk feet forward until hips are over elbows — hold 5 breaths. Walk back. × 3 rounds.
- **Three-Legged Down Dog** (right leg up) → drive knee to nose × 5 → kick up high → hold 3 breaths → lower. Left side × same.

### Handstand Work (15 min)
- **L-shape at wall**: Hands on floor, feet on wall at hip height. "Hips directly over wrists. Press the wall away. Ribs draw in."  Hold 8 breaths × 3.
- **Wall hops from Down Dog** right leg: "Kick one leg up, follow with the other. The goal is to feel vertical — even for 1 second." × 8 hops each side.
- **Handstand at wall (stomach to wall)**: Walk feet up, arrive at vertical. 5–10 breaths. "Now — take one foot off. Hold 3 breaths. Bring it back."
- **Free-standing exploration**: For those ready — kick up from Down Dog with control. "No momentum. The stomach leads. The legs follow."
- **Child's Pose** (10 breaths): Full rest.

### Forearm Balance (Pincha Mayurasana) (10 min)
- **Forearm Plank** × 3 (8 breaths each): Elbows under shoulders, forearms flat. Diagonal line from ears to heels.
- **Pincha Mayurasana prep at wall**: Elbows at wall base, walk feet in, kick up. 8 breaths × 3.
- **Block between hands** (optional): Prevents elbows splaying. "The block maintains the channel. Squeeze it — don't let it fall."
- **Free-standing Pincha Mayurasana**: "Trust the shelf. Press down to rise up."
- **Child's Pose** (10 breaths)

### Counter-Poses & Floor Work (15 min)
- **Setu Bandhasana (Bridge)** × 3 (5 breaths): Reestablish extension after all the shoulder flexion.
- **Paschimottanasana** (2 min): "The spine responds to forward fold after all that extension."
- **Janu Sirsasana** (1 min each side)
- **Supta Baddha Konasana** (Reclined Bound Angle, 3 min): Bolster or folded blanket under the spine — open the chest.
- **Supine Twist** (1 min each side)

### Savasana (8 min)
"Every inversion changes something. When you are upside down, perspective is literally inverted. Let this settle into the body. Rest completely — you've given everything."

## Advanced Flow 3: Backbend Immersion (60 minutes)

**Theme:** Thoracic opening, heart courage, the courage to be vulnerable.  
**Tone:** Expansive, courageous, tender. "The backbend is an act of trust — you open what is most protected."  
**Apex:** Urdhva Dhanurasana (Full Wheel) × 3, Ustrasana (Camel) drop-back (optional)

### Opening (5 min)
- **Prone (on belly)** — Baby Cobra pulses × 10: On each inhale, hover the chest. On each exhale, lower. "The spine wakes up gently."
- **Anahatasana (Melting Heart)** (2 min): Hands forward, hips over knees, chest drops. "This is the thoracic spine opening — let it be heavy, let it melt."
- **Threading the needle** (1 min each side): From hands and knees, slide one arm under. Open the shoulder.

### Sun Salutations — Backbend Emphasis (12 min)
- **Sun Sal A × 4**: On every Upward Dog — hold for 3 breaths. "Push the tops of the feet down. Lift the sternum to the ceiling. Thoracic, not lumbar."
- **Sun Sal B × 3**: In Warrior I — reach the arms back slightly into a mild backbend. "The arms frame the sky."

### Prone Backbend Sequence (10 min)
- **Salabhasana (Locust)** × 3 (8 breaths each): Lift chest, arms, and legs simultaneously. "Spread the wings. Everything opens from center."
- **Dhanurasana (Bow)** × 3 (5 breaths each): Reach back for the ankles. Kick into the hands — the chest lifts from the effort. "The effort is symmetric — both legs kick equally."
- **Bhujangasana (Cobra — full expression)** (5 breaths): Hands forward, press to straighten arms, roll the shoulders back and down. Thoracic extension without lumbar compression.
- **Child's Pose** (8 breaths)

### Ustrasana Sequence (10 min)
- **Ustrasana × 3** (8 breaths each, increasing depth): Round 1 — hands on lower back. Round 2 — hands to heels (toes tucked). Round 3 — tops of feet flat (maximum hip flexor stretch). "Between rounds — take Vajrasana (thunderbolt seat) and feel the echo."
- **Wild Thing** (Camatkarasana) × 2 (5 breaths each side): From Down Dog, flip one side, open the chest. "Let it be wild — let it be unexpected."
- **Child's Pose** (10 breaths)

### Wheel Sequence (10 min)
- **Bridge × 2** (8 breaths each): Establish spinal extension from the floor. Interlace hands below the back — roll the shoulders under.
- **Urdhva Dhanurasana (Full Wheel) × 3**: Come up on an inhale. Rounds 1–2: establish the shape (5 breaths each). Round 3: walk the hands toward the feet, crown on mat — hold 3 breaths, walk back out. "Each round, you understand something new about your spine."
- *Optional for experienced practitioners:* **Camel drop-back**: From standing, reach back to the floor. "This is only for those whose body knows the shape."
- **Counter: Draw knees to chest** — rock gently (1 min).

### Cool-Down (10 min)
- **Paschimottanasana** (2 min): "The most important pose after backbends. Let the spine long-fold."
- **Supta Matsyendrasana** (1.5 min each side): "Wring out. Let the twist undo the backbend."
- **Supta Padangusthasana I** (1 min each side): Hamstring release.
- **Suptam Baddha Konasana** (2 min): Open the chest, hips heavy.

### Savasana (8 min)
"The backbend opens what is closed. The Savasana receives what is open. Everything you offered in this practice — now let the body absorb it."

## Advanced Flow 4: Hanumanasana (Full Splits) Journey (75 minutes)

**Theme:** Patience, persistence, surrender. The splits cannot be forced — only invited.  
**Tone:** Meditative, precise, long holds. "We are building a conversation with the hamstrings and hip flexors — this takes time."  
**Apex:** Hanumanasana (Full Splits)

### Opening (5 min)
- **Seated meditation** (2 min): Sit with legs extended (Dandasana). Feel the hamstrings without pulling. Just notice.
- **Reclined Hamstring stretch** (Supta Padangusthasana I, 2 min each side): Strap or hand to the big toe. "This is the opening conversation."
- **Happy Baby** (1 min): Wide the knees, feel the inner groin.

### Sun Salutations — Hip Flexor Focus (12 min)
- **Sun Sal A × 4**: In each Down Dog, add a Three-Legged Dog with a long hold. Feel the hip flexor of the lifted leg engage.
- **Sun Sal B × 4**: Before each Warrior I, add a Low Lunge breath (from Chaturanga → Up Dog → 3-legged Dog → low lunge). "Breathe 5 breaths in the lunge — the hip flexor is receiving the first deep opening."

### Standing Hamstring Sequence (12 min)
Right side, then Vinyasa, then left.
- **Warrior I** (5 breaths) → **Pyramid Pose** (Parsvottanasana, 8 breaths): Hips squared. Front heel presses, front hamstring lengthens. "Walk the hands back. Fold from the hips, not the waist."
- **Warrior III** (5 breaths) → **Standing Split** (Urdhva Prasarita Eka Padasana, 10 breaths): Let the head hang. Walk the hands back toward the foot. "The standing leg is the anchor. The lifted leg reaches without forcing."
- **Intense Side Stretch into full extension** (5 breaths)
- **Vinyasa**

### Deep Hip Flexor Sequence (12 min)
Right side, then Vinyasa, then left.
- **Anjaneyasana (Low Lunge)** (8 breaths): Back knee down, sink hips forward and down.
- **Lizard (Utthan Pristhasana)** (8 breaths): Both hands inside the front foot. "If the back knee is lifted — find Mula bandha. If it's down — soften everything."
- **Lizard with twist** (5 breaths): Inner hand stays, outer arm rotates open.
- **Half Splits / Ardha Hanumanasana** (8 breaths): Rock between Lizard and Half Splits. "Each exhale — a little more length."
- **Vinyasa**

### Hanumanasana Exploration (15 min)
- **Hanumanasana right** — with blocks as needed (2–3 min): "There is no 'full' version — there is only *your* version today. Use the blocks without apology. They're not a crutch — they're intelligent props."
  - Begin with blocks under hands.
  - On each exhale, let the hips sink a little more.
  - If the floor arrives: release the hands, reach arms overhead. "Feel the full extension — front body open, back body engaged."
- **Come out slowly**: Tuck back toes, rise back to Low Lunge. Feel the hamstring echo.
- **Paschimottanasana** (1 min): Let the hamstring integrate.
- **Hanumanasana left** (2–3 min): same approach.
- **Paschimottanasana** (1 min).

### Floor Cool-Down (10 min)
- **Upavistha Konasana** (Wide-Angle Seated Forward Fold, 2 min): "Gravity. Patience. Breath."
- **Baddha Konasana** (Bound Angle, 2 min): Sole-to-sole. "The inner groin releases last."
- **Supta Matsyendrasana** (1 min each side)
- **Viparita Karani** (Legs up the wall, 3 min): "Let the blood return from the work. Feel the nerves quiet."

### Savasana (7 min)
"Hanumanasana is named for Hanuman — the devotee who leaps without fear. You practiced that today: the willingness to meet your edge, to surrender to where you are, to trust. Rest in that."

## Vinyasa Transition (Linking Breath)

The Vinyasa is the breath-synchronized transition used throughout flowing yoga:

1. **Exhale** → Chaturanga Dandasana (lower halfway — elbows graze the ribs)
2. **Inhale** → Urdhva Mukha Svanasana (Upward Dog: roll over toes, chest forward, thighs lift)
3. **Exhale** → Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward Dog: hips back and up, heels toward floor)

**Advanced Vinyasa quality markers:**
- Chaturanga: elbows stay in — no winging. Shoulders never dip below the elbows.
- Upward Dog: thighs completely off the floor — not Cobra. Pelvis neutral.
- Down Dog: full press through the fingers — index finger base knuckles press first.
- The whole transition takes 3 breaths and is executed with the same focus as any pose.

**Float-back option (advanced):** From standing forward fold — Bahya Kumbhaka (empty exhale) + full Uddiyana → feet float back, land in Chaturanga without a sound.

## Flow Principles for AI Lesson Generation

When building an advanced yoga lesson:

1. **Opening phase** (8% of time): Arrive, breathe, minimal cueing. Advanced practitioners need less instruction to arrive.
2. **Sun Salutations** (18%): Foundation rounds — A then B. Increase pace over rounds. Optional jump-back/float from round 3.
3. **Targeted preparation** (20%): 3–4 poses specifically preparing the apex pose's anatomical demands.
4. **Core/bandha work** (8%): Navasana, Plank variations, Side Plank — build the engine for arm balances or transitions.
5. **Peak** (20%): Apex pose(s). 3–5 rounds of deep exploration. Quality over quantity.
6. **Counter-poses** (12%): Precise counter for the apex category (forward fold for backbend; twist for side work; bridge for hip openers).
7. **Floor restorative** (9%): Progressively quieter. Supta poses. Legs up the wall optional.
8. **Savasana** (8%, minimum 5 min): For advanced practice — longer Savasana. The integration is as important as the work.

**Advanced sequencing rules:**
- Never repeat the same apex family two flows in a row (e.g., after a backbend class, do forward folds next time).
- Warm the specific tissues before the apex — shoulder warm-up before inversions, psoas before splits.
- Follow backbends with forward folds (always). Follow inversions with Child's Pose.
- Silence during peak poses — resist cueing over the practitioner's experience.
- The energetic arc: ignition → building → peak → dissolve → stillness.
- Savasana is never shortened. It completes the arc.

### Opening (3 min)
- **Seated Meditation** (Sukhasana): Come to a comfortable seat. Close the eyes. Take 3 deep breaths, inviting presence. Set a simple intention for your practice — one word or feeling.
- **Neck rolls**: Slowly roll the head to the right, then left. 3x each direction.
- **Cat-Cow** (Bitilasana-Marjaryasana), 6 rounds: On hands and knees. Inhale — arch the back, lift the tailbone and chest (Cow). Exhale — round the spine toward the ceiling, tuck the chin (Cat). Let the breath lead the movement.

### Warm-Up (5 min)
- **Thread the Needle**: From hands and knees, slide the right arm under the body to the left. Hold 5 breaths. Release, switch sides.
- **Downward Dog**: Walk back to Down Dog, pedal the heels, take 5 breaths.
- **Child's Pose**: Walk the hands forward, sink back to Child's Pose. 5 breaths.
- **Sun Salutation A × 2 rounds** (modified: step back, Cobra instead of Up Dog).

### Standing (7 min)
- **High Lunge** (right side, 5 breaths): Step right foot forward, arms up. Feel the hip flexor stretch in the back leg.
- **Warrior II** (right, 5 breaths): Open to the side, arms wide. Gaze over the right fingertips. "Root down to rise up."
- **Triangle Pose** (right, 5 breaths): Straighten the front leg, reach forward and down.
- **Return to Down Dog** → repeat Left side.

### Cool-Down (4 min)
- **Seated Forward Fold** (Paschimottanasana, 10 breaths): Hinge from the hips, spine long.
- **Supine Twist** (1 min each side): Draw one knee across. "Let the exhale carry you deeper."
- **Happy Baby** (1 min): Rock gently.

### Savasana (2 min)
- Lie back, let the body completely release. "You've done enough."

## Complete Flow 2: Intermediate Hip-Opening Flow (45 minutes)

**Theme:** Releasing held tension in the hips, finding freedom in the body and mind.  
**Tone:** Warm, steady. "The hips store our stories — this is a safe place to let them go."

### Opening & Centering (5 min)
- **Supported Child's Pose** (2 min): Arms extended, forehead resting. Allow the body to arrive.
- **3-Part Breath** (2 min): Fill the belly, ribs, and chest in sequence. Exhale fully. Slow the nervous system.
- **Intention setting**: "Choose one thing you're releasing today."
- **Cat-Cow** (8 rounds): Add lateral movement — shift hips side to side. Explore the body's edges.

### Sun Salutations (10 min)
- **Sun Sal A × 3**: Emphasize the hip flexor stretch in the step-backs. "Notice any tightness without judgment."
- **Sun Sal B × 3**: Add a breath in Low Lunge before each Warrior I. "Breathe space into the hips."

### Standing Sequence (15 min)
Perform right side, then left.

- **Warrior II** (8 breaths): Ground the back foot. "What does strength feel like from the inside?"
- **Reverse Warrior** (5 breaths): Arc back, create length in the side waist.
- **Extended Side Angle** (8 breaths): Forearm on thigh or hand to a block. Stack the shoulders.
- **Vinyasa** (Chaturanga → Up Dog → Down Dog)
- **Warrior I** (5 breaths): Hips square forward. "Equal effort in both legs."
- **Warrior III** (5 breaths): Hinge forward, extend the back leg. "Find your horizon line."
- **Standing Split** (5 breaths): Forward fold with one leg lifted. Let the head hang.
- **Vinyasa** back to Down Dog.

### Peak Hip Openers (10 min)
- **Pigeon Pose right** (2–3 min): Walk hands forward, rest on forearms or a block. "This is a surrender pose. You don't need to do anything except breathe and let go."
- **Transition**: Downward Dog.
- **Pigeon Pose left** (2–3 min).
- **Lizard Pose** right (1 min), both hands inside the foot. Option: lower the back knee.
- **Lizard Pose** left (1 min).

### Cool-Down & Floor (5 min)
- **Bridge Pose × 3** (5 breaths each): Activate the glutes and hamstrings, open the hip flexors. Lower slowly.
- **Supine Figure Four / Reclined Pigeon** (1 min each side): Flex the feet, draw the knees in. "The hip tells a story here — just listen."
- **Happy Baby** (1 min).

### Savasana (5 min)
- "Let the work dissolve. Let the body be held by the earth."

## Complete Flow 3: Full Vinyasa Practice (60 minutes)

**Theme:** Building heat, finding your edge, and returning home.  
**Tone:** Energetic for the first half, progressively softer. "We build a fire, then we let it become warmth."

### Opening (5 min)
- **Sukhasana seated meditation** (2 min): Arrive. Notice the quality of your breath — don't change it yet, just notice.
- **Ujjayi breath introduction** (1 min): "Narrow the throat slightly, as if fogging a mirror with your mouth closed. Create an ocean sound."
- **Cat-Cow** (10 rounds): Begin linking breath and movement.
- **Downward Dog** (5 breaths): First Down Dog. "Pedal the heels, take an inventory of the body."

### Sun Salutations (12 min)
- **Sun Sal A × 5**: First round slow. Rounds 2–5 build rhythm. "Let the breath be the leader."
- **Sun Sal B × 4**: Add the full breath in Warrior I. "Feel the warrior arise in you."

### Standing Peak Sequence (18 min)
Right side, vinyasa transition, left side.

- **Warrior II** → **Reverse Warrior** → **Extended Side Angle** → **Triangle** (2 breaths each, flow)
- **Vinyasa**
- **Half Moon** (5 breaths): Challenge the balance. "Soft gaze, strong core."
- **Warrior I** → **Warrior III** → **Standing Split** (flow)
- **Vinyasa**
- **Chair Pose** (8 breaths) → **Eagle Pose** (8 breaths each side)
- **Dancer Pose** (5 breaths each side)

### Core Work (5 min)
- **Plank** (5 breaths): "Build your base."
- **Side Plank right** (Vasisthasana, 5 breaths): Reach the top arm up.
- **Plank** (3 breaths)
- **Side Plank left** (5 breaths)
- **Boat Pose** (Navasana, 5 breaths × 3): Rest in a straight-legged lowering between rounds.

### Backbend Sequence (8 min)
- **Locust Pose** (Salabhasana, 5 breaths × 2): Lie on belly, lift chest, legs, and arms. "Spread the wings."
- **Bow Pose** (Dhanurasana, 5 breaths × 2): Reach back for the ankles. Rock on the belly.
- **Bridge Pose** × 3 (5 breaths each).
- **Wheel Pose** (Urdhva Dhanurasana, 3–5 breaths × 2 if accessible, otherwise additional Bridges).
- **Counter-pose**: Bring knees to chest, rock side to side.

### Cool-Down (8 min)
- **Seated Forward Fold** (2 min): Long spine, surrender on the exhale.
- **Head-to-Knee Pose** (1 min each side).
- **Half Lord of the Fishes Twist** (1 min each side): "Wring out the spine."
- **Reclined Pigeon** (1 min each side).
- **Supine Twist** (1 min each side): "Complete letting go."

### Savasana (5 min)
- Guide slowly: "Let the floor take your full weight. Every muscle softens. Every thought passes like clouds. You have done the work — now rest in who you already are."

## Vinyasa Transition (Linking Breath)

The Vinyasa is the breath-synchronized transition used throughout flowing yoga:

1. **Exhale** → Chaturanga Dandasana (lower halfway)
2. **Inhale** → Urdhva Mukha Svanasana (Upward Dog: roll over toes, chest forward)
3. **Exhale** → Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward Dog: hips back and up)

## Flow Principles for AI Lesson Generation

When building a yoga lesson:

1. **Opening phase** (10% of time): Ground in, breathe, set intention, gentle movement.
2. **Warm-up** (15%): Cat-Cow, spinal mobility, Sun Sal A (gentle pace).
3. **Building heat** (20%): Sun Sal B, standing warriors.
4. **Peak** (25%): Strongest/most challenging poses for the day's theme.
5. **Cool-down** (15%): Counter-poses, forward folds, hip openers, twists.
6. **Restorative** (5%): 1–2 gentle poses, prepare the body for rest.
7. **Savasana** (10%, minimum 3 min): Never less than 3 minutes, ideally 5–10.

**Pose sequencing rules:**
- Always warm up the area before targeting it deeply (e.g., Sun Sals before Pigeon).
- Follow backbends with a gentle twist and/or forward fold as a counter-pose.
- Follow inversions with a few breaths in a neutral position.
- End with cooling, not heating, poses.
- The final poses should be on the floor, progressively quieter.
- Savasana is non-negotiable.
