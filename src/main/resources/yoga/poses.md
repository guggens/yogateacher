# Yoga Poses Reference

Each entry: Sanskrit name · German name · category · setup & alignment cues · common mistakes · modifications · benefits · hold duration.

---

## Mountain Pose
**Sanskrit:** Tadasana  
**German:** Bergpose  
**Category:** Standing, Foundation  
**Setup:** Stand with feet hip-width apart or together. Ground down through all four corners of the feet. Engage the thighs, lift the kneecaps. Lengthen the tailbone down, lift the chest. Arms alongside the body, palms facing forward. Crown of the head rises toward the ceiling.  
**Alignment:** Ears over shoulders, shoulders over hips, hips over ankles. Chin parallel to the floor.  
**Common mistakes:** Locking the knees, hyperextending the lower back, jutting the chin forward.  
**Modifications:** Feet hip-width apart for more stability.  
**Benefits:** Improves posture, body awareness, and grounding. Foundation for all standing poses.  
**Hold:** 5–10 breaths

---

## Forward Fold
**Sanskrit:** Uttanasana  
**German:** Stehende Vorbeuge  
**Category:** Standing, Forward Fold  
**Setup:** From Tadasana, hinge at the hips (not the waist). Bring hands to the floor, shins, or blocks. Let the head hang heavy. Weight shifts slightly forward toward the balls of the feet.  
**Alignment:** Microbend in the knees to protect the hamstrings. Engage the quads to encourage the hamstrings to release.  
**Common mistakes:** Rounding from the lower back instead of hinging from the hips. Forcing the head toward the knees.  
**Modifications:** Bend the knees generously. Use blocks under the hands. Clasp opposite elbows and sway gently (Ragdoll).  
**Benefits:** Lengthens hamstrings and spine, calms the nervous system, releases neck tension.  
**Hold:** 5–10 breaths

---

## Halfway Lift
**Sanskrit:** Ardha Uttanasana  
**German:** Halbe Vorbeuge  
**Category:** Standing, Transition  
**Setup:** From Forward Fold, place hands on shins or blocks. Lengthen the spine forward, lifting the chest. Back becomes flat, parallel to the floor.  
**Alignment:** Shoulders away from the ears. Engage the core. Gaze slightly forward.  
**Common mistakes:** Over-arching the lower back, shrugging the shoulders.  
**Modifications:** Hands on blocks or thighs to make it accessible.  
**Benefits:** Elongates the spine, teaches proper hinging mechanics.  
**Hold:** 1 breath (transition)

---

## Low Lunge
**Sanskrit:** Anjaneyasana  
**German:** Tiefer Ausfallschritt  
**Category:** Standing, Hip Opener  
**Setup:** From Downward Dog, step one foot forward between the hands. Lower the back knee to the mat. Sink the hips toward the floor. Arms can be on the thigh, extended overhead, or in prayer at the chest.  
**Alignment:** Front knee directly over the front ankle. Hips square to the front. Back foot flexed or top of foot on the mat.  
**Common mistakes:** Front knee caving inward, back hip hiking up, crunching the lower back.  
**Modifications:** Pad the back knee. Hands on blocks for height.  
**Benefits:** Deep hip flexor stretch (psoas), opens the front of the thigh and groin.  
**Hold:** 5–10 breaths

---

## High Lunge (Crescent Lunge)
**Sanskrit:** Ashta Chandrasana  
**German:** Hoher Ausfallschritt  
**Category:** Standing, Strength, Hip Opener  
**Setup:** From Low Lunge, tuck the back toes and lift the back knee. Back leg is straight and strong. Arms sweep up overhead, biceps by the ears.  
**Alignment:** Front knee over ankle (90°). Back heel presses back. Core engaged, tailbone lengthens down. Gaze forward or slightly up.  
**Common mistakes:** Front knee beyond the ankle, collapsing into the lower back, shoulders by the ears.  
**Modifications:** Shorten the stance. Hands on hips or heart center.  
**Benefits:** Builds leg strength, stretches hip flexors, improves balance and focus.  
**Hold:** 5–10 breaths

---

## Warrior I
**Sanskrit:** Virabhadrasana I  
**German:** Krieger I  
**Category:** Standing, Strength  
**Setup:** From High Lunge, turn the back foot out 45°, grounding it flat. Hips rotate to face forward. Arms extended overhead, palms facing each other or pressing together.  
**Alignment:** Front knee over ankle. Back foot is planted. Hips square forward — the challenge of the pose. Torso upright, chest open.  
**Common mistakes:** Back hip rotating open, back knee soft, arching the lower back.  
**Modifications:** Shorten the stance. Hands on hips.  
**Benefits:** Builds strength in legs and core, opens chest and hips, cultivates determination (the warrior spirit).  
**Hold:** 5–8 breaths

---

## Warrior II
**Sanskrit:** Virabhadrasana II  
**German:** Krieger II  
**Category:** Standing, Strength  
**Setup:** Stand with feet wide (about 4 feet). Turn the right foot 90° out, left foot 15° in. Bend the right knee to 90°. Arms extend to the sides, parallel to the floor. Gaze over the right fingertips.  
**Alignment:** Front knee tracking over the middle toe. Back leg straight. Torso centered over the hips (not leaning forward). Shoulders relaxed.  
**Common mistakes:** Front knee collapsing inward, torso leaning forward, shoulders raised.  
**Modifications:** Shorten the stance. Reduce the knee bend.  
**Benefits:** Strengthens legs, opens hips and chest, builds stamina and focus.  
**Hold:** 5–10 breaths

---

## Warrior III
**Sanskrit:** Virabhadrasana III  
**German:** Krieger III  
**Category:** Standing, Balance  
**Setup:** From High Lunge or Tadasana, hinge forward at the hips while simultaneously lifting the back leg. Body forms a "T" shape: torso and back leg parallel to the floor. Arms extend forward, alongside the body, or in prayer.  
**Alignment:** Hips level (avoid letting the lifted-leg hip open). Standing knee slightly bent. Core strongly engaged. Gaze at the floor.  
**Common mistakes:** Hips uneven, collapsing in the standing leg, looking up and losing balance.  
**Modifications:** Hands on a wall or blocks. Standing leg bent more.  
**Benefits:** Builds exceptional balance, core strength, and full-body coordination.  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths each side

---

## Reverse Warrior
**Sanskrit:** Viparita Virabhadrasana  
**German:** Umgekehrter Krieger  
**Category:** Standing, Side Body  
**Setup:** From Warrior II, flip the front palm up and arc the front arm up and back. Back hand rests lightly on the back thigh. Side body opens into a gentle backbend.  
**Alignment:** Keep the front knee bent and stable. Reach through the fingertips, don't collapse into the lower back.  
**Common mistakes:** Putting weight onto the back hand, collapsing the front knee.  
**Benefits:** Opens the side waist, strengthens the legs, energizing transition in a Vinyasa flow.  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths

---

## Extended Side Angle
**Sanskrit:** Utthita Parsvakonasana  
**German:** Gestreckter Seitenwinkel  
**Category:** Standing, Side Body  
**Setup:** From Warrior II, bring the front forearm to the front thigh (or hand to the floor/block outside the front foot). Top arm extends overhead, creating one long line from the back foot to the fingertips.  
**Alignment:** Both sides of the waist long. Top shoulder rolling open. Gaze up at the top hand.  
**Common mistakes:** Dumping the front shoulder, compressing the side waist.  
**Modifications:** Forearm on thigh. Block under the hand.  
**Benefits:** Intense side-body stretch, hip opening, strengthens legs and core.  
**Hold:** 5–8 breaths

---

## Triangle Pose
**Sanskrit:** Trikonasana  
**German:** Dreieckspose  
**Category:** Standing, Side Body  
**Setup:** Wide-legged stance, front foot 90° out. Front leg stays straight. Hinge sideways, bringing the front hand to the shin, block, or floor. Top arm reaches to the ceiling.  
**Alignment:** Both legs straight (microbend in front knee if tight). Torso rotates open. Stack the hips. Gaze up, sideways, or down.  
**Common mistakes:** Rounding the torso forward rather than rotating open, locking the front knee.  
**Modifications:** Block under the front hand. Bend the front knee slightly.  
**Benefits:** Stretches hamstrings, hips, and thoracic spine. Opens the chest. Grounds and stabilizes.  
**Hold:** 5–8 breaths

---

## Half Moon Pose
**Sanskrit:** Ardha Chandrasana  
**German:** Halbmondpose  
**Category:** Standing, Balance  
**Setup:** From Triangle, bend the front knee slightly and bring the front hand to the floor or a block about 12" in front of the foot. Shift weight forward, lifting the back leg to hip height. Top arm reaches up. Open the torso like a book.  
**Alignment:** Standing leg is straight (or microbent). Lifted leg is parallel to the floor with foot flexed. Hips stack. Gaze up, sideways, or down.  
**Common mistakes:** Collapsing in the standing hip, gaze pulling the body forward.  
**Modifications:** Block under the hand. Practice with back against a wall.  
**Benefits:** Advanced balance, hip opening, strengthens the entire leg and core.  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths

---

## Chair Pose
**Sanskrit:** Utkatasana  
**German:** Stuhlpose / Kräftige Hocke  
**Category:** Standing, Strength  
**Setup:** From Tadasana, bend the knees as if sitting into an imaginary chair. Arms sweep up overhead. Weight stays in the heels.  
**Alignment:** Knees tracking over toes (not beyond them). Torso leans slightly forward but spine is long. Shoulders relaxed, tailbone pointing down.  
**Common mistakes:** Knees caving inward, excessive lower back arch.  
**Modifications:** Feet hip-width apart. Less depth in the squat.  
**Benefits:** Strengthens thighs, glutes, calves, and core. Builds heat and determination.  
**Hold:** 5–8 breaths

---

## Goddess Pose
**Sanskrit:** Utkata Konasana  
**German:** Göttin  
**Category:** Standing, Strength, Hip Opener  
**Setup:** Wide stance, toes turned out 45°. Bend the knees to 90° (as much as comfortable), thighs parallel to the floor. Arms in cactus (90° bend), palms forward.  
**Alignment:** Knees track over toes. Torso upright. Engage the inner thighs.  
**Modifications:** Less depth in the squat.  
**Benefits:** Opens inner thighs and hips, strengthens legs, connects to feminine energy and power.  
**Hold:** 5–8 breaths

---

## Standing Split
**Sanskrit:** Urdhva Prasarita Eka Padasana  
**German:** Stehender Spagat  
**Category:** Standing, Balance, Hamstring  
**Setup:** From Forward Fold, shift weight to one foot and slowly lift the other leg as high as comfortable behind you. Hands stay on the floor or grasp the standing ankle. Gaze at the standing calf.  
**Alignment:** Hips level (avoid opening the hip of the lifted leg). Standing leg straight or slightly bent.  
**Modifications:** Hands on blocks. Reduce height of lifted leg.  
**Benefits:** Deep hamstring stretch, balance, inversion benefits from the folded torso.  
**Hold:** 5–8 breaths

---

## Downward-Facing Dog
**Sanskrit:** Adho Mukha Svanasana  
**German:** Herabschauender Hund  
**Category:** Foundation, Inversion, Transition  
**Setup:** From hands and knees, tuck the toes and lift the hips up and back. Body forms an inverted V. Hands shoulder-width, feet hip-width. Press the floor away, lengthen the spine.  
**Alignment:** Fingers spread wide, index fingers parallel. Arms straight. Hips high. Heels reach toward (not necessarily to) the floor. Head between the arms, ears level with upper arms.  
**Common mistakes:** Rounding the spine, dumping into the wrists, bending the knees excessively.  
**Modifications:** Bent knees to lengthen the spine first. Hands on blocks.  
**Benefits:** Full-body stretch (hamstrings, calves, spine, shoulders). Gentle inversion. Central pose of Vinyasa yoga.  
**Hold:** 5 breaths (or as a rest pose)

---

## Plank Pose
**Sanskrit:** Phalakasana  
**German:** Plankpose / Stützpose  
**Category:** Core, Strength  
**Setup:** From Downward Dog, shift forward until shoulders are over wrists. Body forms a straight line from heels to crown.  
**Alignment:** Core engaged, hips level (not sagging or piking). Shoulders draw back and down. Gaze at the floor.  
**Common mistakes:** Hips too high or too low, wrists misaligned under shoulders.  
**Modifications:** Knees on the mat (low plank).  
**Benefits:** Builds core, arm, and shoulder strength. Foundational for Chaturanga.  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths

---

## Four-Limbed Staff Pose (Low Push-Up)
**Sanskrit:** Chaturanga Dandasana  
**German:** Vier-Glieder-Stab-Pose  
**Category:** Core, Strength, Transition  
**Setup:** From Plank, shift forward on the toes, bend the elbows to 90°, hugging them into the ribs. Lower halfway — body hovers parallel to the floor.  
**Alignment:** Elbows at 90°, not flaring out. Shoulders at the same height as the elbows (not dipping below). Core strongly engaged.  
**Common mistakes:** Elbows flaring wide, shoulders dipping below elbows (strain on the shoulder joint), hips sagging.  
**Modifications:** Lower the knees first. Practice against a wall.  
**Benefits:** Builds tremendous upper-body and core strength. Key pose in Vinyasa sequences.  
**Hold:** 1 breath (transition)

---

## Upward-Facing Dog
**Sanskrit:** Urdhva Mukha Svanasana  
**German:** Aufwärtsschauender Hund  
**Category:** Backbend, Chest Opener  
**Setup:** From Chaturanga, roll over the toes, press into the hands, and straighten the arms. Hips and thighs lift off the mat. Chest opens forward and up.  
**Alignment:** Shoulders back and down, away from the ears. Wrists under the shoulders. Thighs and feet off the mat. Gaze forward or slightly up.  
**Common mistakes:** Compressing the lower back (lack of core engagement), shoulders by the ears.  
**Modifications:** Cobra pose (Bhujangasana) as a gentler alternative.  
**Benefits:** Opens the chest, strengthens the back, stretches the front body.  
**Hold:** 1 breath (transition)

---

## Cobra Pose
**Sanskrit:** Bhujangasana  
**German:** Kobra  
**Category:** Backbend, Gentle  
**Setup:** Lie on the belly. Hands under the shoulders, elbows close to the ribs. Press gently through the hands and lift the chest. Elbows may remain slightly bent.  
**Alignment:** Pubic bone stays on the mat. Shoulders down and back. Gaze forward.  
**Common mistakes:** Straightening the arms and crunching the lower back, lifting too high.  
**Benefits:** Gentle backbend, opens the chest, strengthens the back muscles. Good for beginners.  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths

---

## Camel Pose
**Sanskrit:** Ustrasana  
**German:** Kamelpose  
**Category:** Backbend, Chest Opener  
**Setup:** Kneel on the mat, knees hip-width. Place hands on the lower back, fingers pointing down. Press the hips forward, open the chest up. For full expression, reach hands back to the heels.  
**Alignment:** Hips directly over the knees. Avoid compressing the lower back — use core engagement. Head can drop back if the neck is comfortable.  
**Common mistakes:** Crunching the lumbar spine, collapsing the chest.  
**Modifications:** Curl the toes under (raises the heels, easier to reach). Stay with hands on lower back.  
**Benefits:** Deep front-body opening, energizing, counters the effects of sitting.  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths

---

## Bridge Pose
**Sanskrit:** Setu Bandha Sarvangasana  
**German:** Brücke  
**Category:** Backbend, Hip Opener  
**Setup:** Lie on the back, knees bent, feet flat on the floor hip-width apart and close to the hips. Press into the feet, lift the hips. Clasp the hands beneath the body and roll the shoulders under.  
**Alignment:** Knees directly over ankles. Thighs parallel. Press evenly through the feet. Chin slightly tucked.  
**Common mistakes:** Feet splaying out, knees collapsing in, crunching the neck.  
**Modifications:** Block under the sacrum for a restorative variation.  
**Benefits:** Strengthens the glutes and hamstrings, opens the chest, gentle spinal extension.  
**Hold:** 5–8 breaths

---

## Wheel Pose (Full Backbend)
**Sanskrit:** Urdhva Dhanurasana  
**German:** Rad / Voller Rückbeuger  
**Category:** Backbend, Advanced  
**Setup:** Lie on back, bend knees with feet flat. Bring hands by the ears, fingertips pointing toward the feet. Press into hands and feet simultaneously, lift the hips and crown of the head first, then straighten the arms.  
**Alignment:** Feet hip-width and parallel. Elbows tracking toward each other. Weight distributed evenly.  
**Common mistakes:** Feet and knees splaying out, elbows flaring, compressing the neck.  
**Modifications:** Bridge pose. Three bridges as warm-up. Practice lifting just the crown first.  
**Benefits:** Powerful chest and front-body opener, energizing, strengthens arms and legs.  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths

---

## Pigeon Pose
**Sanskrit:** Eka Pada Rajakapotasana  
**German:** Taubenpas / Einbeinige Taube  
**Category:** Hip Opener, Deep  
**Setup:** From Downward Dog, bring the right knee forward behind the right wrist, right shin on an angle. Extend the left leg back straight. Square the hips toward the mat. Walk the hands forward and fold over the front leg.  
**Alignment:** Front shin at whatever angle is comfortable (more parallel to the top = more intense). Back hip may use a blanket if it floats.  
**Common mistakes:** Ankle in a painful position, hip collapsing to the side.  
**Modifications:** Reclined Pigeon (Supta Kapotasana / Figure Four) as an accessible alternative.  
**Benefits:** Intense external hip rotation stretch, releases the piriformis and deep hip rotators. Often emotionally releasing.  
**Hold:** 1–3 minutes each side

---

## Lizard Pose
**Sanskrit:** Utthan Pristhasana  
**German:** Eidechsenpose  
**Category:** Hip Opener  
**Setup:** From Low Lunge, bring both hands to the inside of the front foot. Option to lower the back knee. Open the chest forward.  
**Alignment:** Front knee over ankle or slightly beyond for more intensity. Keep the back leg straight or lower the knee.  
**Modifications:** Hands on blocks. Keep back knee down.  
**Benefits:** Deep hip flexor, groin, and inner thigh stretch.  
**Hold:** 5–10 breaths

---

## Happy Baby
**Sanskrit:** Ananda Balasana  
**German:** Glückliches Baby  
**Category:** Hip Opener, Restorative  
**Setup:** Lie on the back, bring the knees to the chest, then open them wide. Reach up to hold the outer edges of the feet (or ankles, or shins). Gently pull the knees toward the armpits.  
**Alignment:** Lower back presses into the mat. Rock gently side to side if it feels good.  
**Benefits:** Deep inner groin and hip stretch, gentle spine massage, very calming.  
**Hold:** 1–3 minutes

---

## Seated Forward Fold
**Sanskrit:** Paschimottanasana  
**German:** Sitzende Vorbeuge  
**Category:** Forward Fold, Hamstrings  
**Setup:** Sit with legs extended. Flex the feet. Hinge forward from the hips (not the waist), reaching for the feet, shins, or a strap around the feet.  
**Alignment:** Spine long, not rounded. Sit bones grounding into the mat. Pull the toes back.  
**Common mistakes:** Rounding aggressively to reach the toes rather than keeping the spine long.  
**Modifications:** Bent knees. Strap around the feet. Seated on a blanket.  
**Benefits:** Deep hamstring and spine stretch, calming, introspective.  
**Hold:** 1–3 minutes

---

## Head-to-Knee Pose
**Sanskrit:** Janu Sirsasana  
**German:** Kopf-zum-Knie-Pose  
**Category:** Forward Fold, Hamstrings  
**Setup:** Sit with one leg extended, bend the other knee and bring the sole of the foot to the inner thigh. Hinge forward over the extended leg.  
**Alignment:** Torso square to the extended leg. Long spine.  
**Modifications:** Bent extended knee. Strap around the foot.  
**Benefits:** Stretches hamstrings, inner groin, spine. Also a gentle twist.  
**Hold:** 1–3 minutes each side

---

## Supine Twist
**Sanskrit:** Supta Matsyendrasana  
**German:** Liegender Twist  
**Category:** Twist, Restorative  
**Setup:** Lie on the back, draw one knee to the chest and guide it across the body to the opposite side. Extend the same arm out to the side at shoulder height. Gaze in the opposite direction.  
**Alignment:** Both shoulders stay on the mat. Adjust the crossed knee height for different spinal regions.  
**Benefits:** Releases spinal tension, aids digestion, calming. Perfect cool-down pose.  
**Hold:** 1–3 minutes each side

---

## Half Lord of the Fishes
**Sanskrit:** Ardha Matsyendrasana  
**German:** Halber Fischgott-Twist  
**Category:** Seated Twist  
**Setup:** Sit with legs extended. Bend one knee and place the foot outside the opposite thigh. Sit up tall. Hook the opposite elbow outside the raised knee. Other hand behind on the floor. Twist toward the raised knee.  
**Alignment:** Lengthen the spine on the inhale, deepen the twist on the exhale. Both sit bones down.  
**Common mistakes:** Rounding the spine, lifting the sit bone.  
**Benefits:** Deep spinal rotation, detoxifying for the organs, improves spinal mobility.  
**Hold:** 5–10 breaths each side

---

## Boat Pose
**Sanskrit:** Navasana  
**German:** Bootpose  
**Category:** Core, Strength  
**Setup:** Sit with knees bent. Lean back slightly, lift the feet so the shins are parallel to the floor. Extend arms forward. Option to straighten the legs for full expression.  
**Alignment:** Spine tall (not rounded). Core engaged. Gaze forward.  
**Modifications:** Keep knees bent. Hold behind the thighs for support.  
**Benefits:** Strengthens the core, hip flexors, and spine. Builds concentration.  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths, repeat 3x

## Child's Pose
**Sanskrit:** Balasana  
**German:** Kindshaltung / Kindpose  
**Category:** Restorative, Rest  
**Setup:** From hands and knees, bring big toes together, knees as wide as comfortable. Sink the hips toward the heels. Extend arms forward or alongside the body. Forehead to the mat.  
**Alignment:** Hips toward heels (supported with a blanket if they don't reach). Spine long and relaxed.  
**Modifications:** Arms alongside body (passive). Blanket under the hips. Blanket under the forehead.  
**Benefits:** Resting pose, grounding, releases the back and hips. Use any time during practice.  
**Hold:** As long as needed — 5 breaths to several minutes

## Legs-Up-The-Wall
**Sanskrit:** Viparita Karani  
**German:** Beine-an-der-Wand  
**Category:** Restorative, Inversion  
**Setup:** Sit sideways to a wall, then swing the legs up the wall as you lower the torso to the floor. Hips close to or against the wall.  
**Alignment:** Legs relaxed, feet soft. Arms open to the sides.  
**Benefits:** Deeply restorative, reverses blood flow, reduces swelling in the legs, calms the nervous system.  
**Hold:** 5–15 minutes

---

## Corpse Pose / Final Relaxation
**Sanskrit:** Savasana  
**German:** Totenstellung / Entspannungspose  
**Category:** Restorative, Final Relaxation  
**Setup:** Lie flat on the back. Let the feet fall open. Arms slightly away from the body, palms facing up. Close the eyes. Completely surrender all effort.  
**Alignment:** Body symmetric. Jaw unclenched. Eyes closed and soft.  
**Modifications:** Blanket under the knees for lower back comfort. Eye pillow. Warm blanket over the body.  
**Benefits:** Integration of the practice. Activates the parasympathetic nervous system. Essential — never skip Savasana.  
**Hold:** 5–15 minutes

## Tree Pose
**Sanskrit:** Vrksasana  
**German:** Baumpose  
**Category:** Balance  
**Setup:** Stand in Tadasana. Shift weight to one foot. Place the sole of the other foot on the inner ankle, inner calf, or inner thigh (avoid the knee joint). Arms can be in prayer at the chest, extended overhead, or wide like branches.  
**Alignment:** Standing leg straight, hip open. Core engaged. Gaze at a fixed point (drishti) at eye level.  
**Common mistakes:** Foot pressing into the knee joint, hip of lifted leg shooting out.  
**Modifications:** Toes on the floor with heel at ankle for more stability.  
**Benefits:** Improves balance, focus, and ankle stability. Grounding and calming.  
**Hold:** 5–10 breaths each side

---

## Eagle Pose
**Sanskrit:** Garudasana  
**German:** Adlerpose  
**Category:** Balance, Hip Opener  
**Setup:** Bend the standing knee slightly. Wrap one leg over the other, hooking the top foot behind the calf if possible. Wrap the arms: one elbow under the other, forearms crossing, palms touching.  
**Alignment:** Elbows at shoulder height. Sink the hips lower. Gaze forward.  
**Common mistakes:** Standing leg too straight, torso pitching forward.  
**Modifications:** Toes on the floor instead of wrapping. Simple arm cross.  
**Benefits:** Opens the hips and shoulder girdle simultaneously, improves balance and concentration.  
**Hold:** 5–8 breaths each side

---

## Dancer Pose
**Sanskrit:** Natarajasana  
**German:** Tänzerpas / Tanzpose  
**Category:** Balance, Backbend  
**Setup:** Stand on one leg. Bend the other knee and grasp the inner ankle with the same-side hand. Kick the foot back and up, simultaneously tilting the torso forward. Top arm extends forward or up.  
**Alignment:** Standing hip stays level. Chest open. Kick against the hand to deepen the backbend.  
**Common mistakes:** Knee of the held leg splaying out (keep it in line), collapsing forward.  
**Modifications:** Stand near a wall. Hold a strap around the foot.  
**Benefits:** Combines balance, backbend, and hip flexor stretch. Graceful and joyful.  
**Hold:** 5–8 breaths each side

---

## Shoulder Stand
**Sanskrit:** Sarvangasana  
**German:** Schulterstand  
**Category:** Inversion  
**Setup:** Lie on the back. Bring knees to the chest and swing the hips overhead. Support the mid-back with the hands. Extend the legs toward the ceiling.  
**Alignment:** Weight on the shoulders and upper arms — NOT the neck. Chin tucked toward chest slightly. Body as vertical as possible.  
**Contraindications:** Avoid with neck injuries. Use supported version (legs on a chair) as alternative.  
**Benefits:** Stimulates the thyroid, calms the nervous system, reverses blood flow.  
**Hold:** 1–5 minutes

---

## Fish Pose
**Sanskrit:** Matsyasana  
**German:** Fischpose  
**Category:** Backbend, Counter-pose  
**Setup:** Lie on the back. Slide the hands under the hips. Press into the elbows and arch the chest up, tilting the head back to rest the crown on the mat.  
**Alignment:** Weight on the elbows (not the head). Chest lifts high.  
**Benefits:** Counter-pose to Shoulder Stand. Opens the throat and chest.  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths

---

## Sleeping Swan / Yin Pigeon
**Sanskrit:** (Yin variation of Eka Pada Rajakapotasana)  
**German:** Schlafender Schwan  
**Category:** Yin, Hip Opener  
**Setup:** Like Pigeon Pose but held passively with props: bolster or folded blankets under the front hip and forehead. No muscular effort — surrender completely.  
**Benefits:** Long-held hip release, stimulates the kidney/liver meridians, deeply meditative.  
**Hold:** 3–5 minutes each side

# ARM BALANCES

---

## Crow Pose
**Sanskrit:** Bakasana  
**German:** Krähenpose  
**Category:** Arm Balance  
**Setup:** Squat deeply (Malasana). Plant hands shoulder-width, spread fingers wide. Bring knees high onto the backs of the upper arms (near the armpits). Shift weight forward until feet lift — hug knees tight to arms. Gaze slightly forward, not down.  
**Key actions:** Uddiyana bandha, round the upper back (cat-spine), elbows squeeze in slightly. The more you round, the lighter the legs feel.  
**Cue:** "Knees squeeze like a vice. Belly draws in and up. The arms are just a shelf."  
**Entry from Down Dog:** Three-legged dog → knee to nose → round the spine forward, shift weight onto hands  
**Exit:** Slowly lower feet, or jump back to Chaturanga (advanced)  
**Hold:** 5–10 breaths

---

## Side Crow
**Sanskrit:** Parsva Bakasana  
**German:** Seitliche Krähenpose  
**Category:** Arm Balance, Twist  
**Setup:** From Malasana or squatting, twist torso deeply to one side. Stack both knees onto the outside of one upper arm. Tilt forward, lift feet.  
**Key actions:** The twist must be in the thoracic spine — lumbar twists don't create the necessary shelf. Stack hips over hands.  
**Cue:** "Rotate from the ribs, not the lower back. Both hips stack — no splaying."  
**Build-up:** Parivrtta Utkatasana (twisted chair) must be solid first.  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths each side

---

## One-Legged Crow / Eka Pada Bakasana
**Sanskrit:** Eka Pada Bakasana  
**German:** Einbeinige Krähenpose  
**Category:** Arm Balance  
**Setup:** From Bakasana (Crow), straighten one leg behind you while maintaining the other knee on the arm. Requires full engagement of both Mula and Uddiyana bandha.  
**Key actions:** The extended leg reaches back actively — don't just drop it. Square the hips.  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths each side

---

## Firefly Pose
**Sanskrit:** Tittibhasana  
**German:** Glühwürmchenpose  
**Category:** Arm Balance  
**Setup:** From a deep forward fold, slide shoulders under the knees (thighs rest on the backs of the upper arms). Straighten the arms, straighten the legs, reach feet away.  
**Key actions:** This requires deep hip flexion AND hamstring length. The hips must be as high as the shoulders to thread through. Core draws in strongly.  
**Cue:** "The hips are the engine. Shoot them back and up."  
**Prerequisite:** Kurmasana (Tortoise) — seated with legs over the shoulders.  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths

## Pose Dedicated to Sage Koundinya I
**Sanskrit:** Eka Pada Koundinyasana I  
**German:** Koundinyasana I  
**Category:** Arm Balance, Twist  
**Setup:** From Parivrtta Lunge or Fallen Triangle — stack the front hip crease onto the outside of the upper arm. Extend both legs in opposite directions (scissored). The twist is extreme.  
**Key actions:** Top hip presses down into the arm shelf. Lower leg reaches down and back, top leg extends forward. "Find the ledge — the hip bone is a hook."  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths each side

## Pose Dedicated to Sage Koundinya II
**Sanskrit:** Eka Pada Koundinyasana II  
**German:** Koundinyasana II  
**Category:** Arm Balance  
**Setup:** From a low lunge or Lizard, bring the front knee/shin to rest on the back of the upper arm (same-side arm). Extend both legs — front leg reaches forward, back leg back.  
**Key actions:** Front leg presses into the arm; back leg extends and lifts. Hips stay level. Strong bandha engagement.  
**Cue:** "Dial the front hip under. Reach both legs away from each other."  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths each side

## Pose Dedicated to Sage Vishvamitra
**Sanskrit:** Vishvamitrasana  
**German:** Vishvamitrasana  
**Category:** Arm Balance, Hip Opener  
**Setup:** From a low lunge, bring the front leg to rest high on the back of the same-side arm (similar to EkaPK II). Rotate the torso, extend the top arm toward the ceiling, then turn the palm down and reach forward.  
**Key actions:** This is a side plank + arm balance + hip opener simultaneously. Requires extreme hip external rotation and hamstring flexibility.  
**Prerequisites:** Vasisthasana, EkaPK II, deep Lizard Pose  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths each side

## Eight-Angle Pose
**Sanskrit:** Astavakrasana  
**German:** Achtwinkelpose  
**Category:** Arm Balance, Twist  
**Setup:** From seated, hook one leg over the shoulder. Cross the ankles. Lean forward, plant hands, extend the legs to one side parallel to the floor.  
**Key actions:** Squeeze thighs around the arm strongly. Rotate the torso slightly in the direction of the legs. Both legs stay straight and extended.  
**Cue:** "The thighs are a clamp. Hug the arm. Rotate from the belly."  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths each side

## Flying Pigeon
**Sanskrit:** Eka Pada Galavasana  
**German:** Fliegende Taubenpose  
**Category:** Arm Balance, Hip Opener  
**Setup:** From Figure Four balance (standing), hook the foot around the arm (foot in the crook of the elbow). Plant both hands, shift forward, extend the back leg.  
**Key actions:** The foot-arm hook is the anchor — flex the foot aggressively. Square the hips as much as possible.  
**Cue:** "Flex the foot like you're pushing a wall. That hip flexion is your shelf."  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths each side

# INVERSIONS

---

## Handstand
**Sanskrit:** Adho Mukha Vrksasana  
**German:** Handstand  
**Category:** Inversion, Arm Balance  
**Setup:** From Down Dog, walk hands in, shift weight forward. Kick one leg up, following with the other (or hop both feet up). Stacked: hands → shoulders → hips → heels. Arms completely straight.  
**Key actions:** Push the floor away (protract shoulders). Spread fingers wide for grip. Engage the core and bandhas — body is one long line. Gaze between the hands.  
**Cue:** "The floor presses back. Resist it. You're not balancing — you're actively pushing."  
**Against wall:** Stomach to wall (back to room) is the safest learning position — prevents banana-back.  
**Free-standing entry:** From Down Dog, shift shoulders over wrists, kick up one leg, control the descent.  
**Hold:** 5–15 breaths (or as long as comfortable)

---

## Forearm Balance / Feathered Peacock
**Sanskrit:** Pincha Mayurasana  
**German:** Unterarmstand / Pfauenpose  
**Category:** Inversion  
**Setup:** From Dolphin Pose (forearms on mat, shoulder-width, fingers spread). Walk feet in until hips are over elbows. Kick one leg up, follow with the other. Forearms press actively into the floor.  
**Key actions:** Elbows stay shoulder-width (use blocks between hands if they splay). Chest opens upward. Strong bandhas throughout. Gaze between the forearms.  
**Cue:** "Elbows as wide as the shoulders — no wider. Press the whole forearm. Reach through the heels."  
**Prerequisite:** Dolphin with straight elbows, held for 30+ seconds.  
**Hold:** 5–15 breaths

---

## Supported Headstand
**Sanskrit:** Salamba Sirsasana I  
**German:** Kopfstand  
**Category:** Inversion  
**Setup:** Interlace fingers, cup the back of the skull, elbows shoulder-width. Top of the crown on the mat (not the forehead). Walk feet in until hips are directly above head. Tuck knees, lift, then straighten legs.  
**Key actions:** 90% weight on forearms, 10% on crown — NEVER compress the neck. Press the forearms down to unload the head.  
**Cue:** "Push down through the forearms. The neck is long, not compressed. The cervical spine is a passenger, not a pillar."  
**Hold:** 5–30 breaths (build slowly over months)  
**Counter-pose:** Always follow with Child's Pose for 10+ breaths.

---

## Tripod Headstand
**Sanskrit:** Salamba Sirsasana II  
**German:** Dreibein-Kopfstand  
**Category:** Inversion  
**Setup:** Hands flat on mat, shoulder-width. Top of the head between the hands (forming a tripod). Tuck knees onto the backs of the arms, then lift. Weight distributed across three points: two hands + crown.  
**Key actions:** Arms form a stable tripod. Core lifts the legs — don't kick up. This is a good gateway to Handstand/arm balances.  
**Hold:** 5–15 breaths

---

## Scorpion
**Sanskrit:** Vrischikasana  
**German:** Skorpionpose  
**Category:** Inversion, Backbend  
**Setup:** From Pincha Mayurasana (Forearm Stand), bend both knees and arch the feet toward the head. The thoracic spine extends deeply.  
**Key actions:** This is an inversion AND a deep backbend simultaneously — the most demanding combination. Thoracic mobility is the limiting factor, not strength.  
**Prerequisites:** Fluent Pincha Mayurasana + Kapotasana-level backbend range.  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths

# ADVANCED BACKBENDS

---

## Full Camel
**Sanskrit:** Ustrasana (full expression)  
**German:** Kamelpose (vollständig)  
**Category:** Backbend  
**Setup:** From kneeling, tuck toes under for height, or tops of feet flat. Hips over knees. Hands reach back to heels. Let the head follow last — don't just drop it back, lengthen the front of the neck first.  
**Key actions:** Push the hips forward (over the knees, not behind). Thoracic extension, not lumbar collapse. The pelvis is neutral.  
**Cue:** "Hips lead the heart. The head comes last."  
**Hold:** 5–8 breaths × 3 rounds

---

## King Pigeon Full Backbend
**Sanskrit:** Kapotasana  
**German:** Königs-Taubenpose  
**Category:** Backbend, Advanced  
**Setup:** From kneeling (knees hip-width), arch back until hands reach the floor behind. Walk hands toward the feet, eventually gripping the feet or ankles.  
**Key actions:** This is one of the deepest backbends in yoga. Requires tremendous thoracic extension — the lumbar spine is not the target. The hip flexors must be open to allow the pelvis to drop forward.  
**Cue:** "The chest reaches for the sky before it reaches for the floor behind you."  
**Prerequisites:** Full Wheel with walking hands in, multiple years of backbend work.  
**Hold:** 3–5 breaths (respect this pose completely)

---

## Full Splits
**Sanskrit:** Hanumanasana  
**German:** Hanuman-Pose / Spagat  
**Category:** Hip Opener, Hamstring Stretch, Backbend  
**Setup:** From Low Lunge, slide the front foot forward and back knee back simultaneously. Use blocks under the front hip as needed. Square the hips. Eventually, the front hip meets the mat.  
**Key actions:** Front hamstring lengthens (fold forward slightly). Back hip flexor opens (tilt the pelvis anteriorly). Hips stay square — do not rotate the pelvis to "get deeper."  
**Cue:** "This is a dialogue, not a destination. Use the blocks. Honor where you are today."  
**Hold:** 30 seconds to 2 minutes each side — gravity is the teacher

---

## Middle Splits
**Sanskrit:** Samakonasana  
**German:** Mittlerer Spagat  
**Category:** Hip Opener, Advanced  
**Setup:** From wide-legged standing, slowly walk the feet out until the inner thighs meet the floor. Support on blocks as needed.  
**Key actions:** Internal hip rotation (toes pointed up or slightly forward). Pelvis neutral. Lengthen the spine to prevent rounding.  
**Prerequisites:** Very open adductors — years of Prasarita Padottanasana and Baddha Konasana  
**Hold:** 1–3 minutes

---

## Tortoise Pose
**Sanskrit:** Kurmasana  
**German:** Schildkrötenpose  
**Category:** Deep Hip / Hamstring, Advanced  
**Setup:** Seated, legs wide. Fold forward and slide arms under the legs — shoulders under the knees. Flatten the back.  
**Key actions:** External hip rotation allows the legs to slide over the shoulders. This is the prerequisite for Tittibhasana.  
**Hold:** 8–10 breaths

---

## Sleeping Tortoise
**Sanskrit:** Supta Kurmasana  
**German:** Liegende Schildkrötenpose  
**Category:** Deep Hip / Hamstring, Advanced  
**Setup:** From Kurmasana, cross the feet behind the head, and clasp the hands behind the back. Forehead rests on the mat.  
**Key actions:** Complete surrender — external rotation + hip flexion + forward fold simultaneously.  
**Note:** One of the most demanding poses in the Primary Series of Ashtanga.  
**Hold:** 5–8 breaths
